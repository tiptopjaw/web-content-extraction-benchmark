Quite a lot of data wrangling to get there,\nthough. \n Step one, get the coordinates. Step 2, generate a list of distances\nbetween each pair. \n It's good to see an actual straight line distance in one of these;\nusually they use Manhattan distance, which I find rather dull. Of\ncourse, because I'm comparing the distances, I don't need to take the\nsquare root. \n Step 3, sort that list and take the top N (10 for the test case, 1000\nfor the live input). (I suspect\nI'm losing a lot of speed here, but it's fast enough for today's\nproblem.) Once all nodes are connected, I look at the nodes making up\nthe latest connection to get my answer. \n (Might spanning tree be the answer after all?
