{"title":"Cybersecurity Becoming Increasingly Important in the Home Building Industry","author":null,"date":"2023-10-20T00:00:00+00:00","main_content":"The toll data breaches are taking on U.S. businesses is on the rise, and construction firms – especially general contractors – are becoming a more popular target for cyber criminals.\n\nThe FBI’s Internet Crime Complaint Center reported that in 2022 it received more than 800,000 complaints of cyber crimes with losses of around $10.3 billion, up sharply from $6.9 billion in 2021. Phishing was the most common type of complaint received with more than 300,000 reported while business email impersonation scams cost companies more than $2.7 billion last year.\n\nA recent survey revealed that 59% of architecture, engineering and construction firms have experienced a cybersecurity threat in the past two years, including 70% of general contractors. Contractors are not seen as the ultimate target of cyber criminals, however. Most attacks are targeting client data.\n\nHome builders are beginning to see this practice firsthand, as a string of recent data breaches has hit the industry.\n\nOctober is Cybersecurity Awareness Month and it comes at the perfect time for NAHB members to review their data privacy and cybersecurity capabilities.\n\nBusinesses need to have a baseline system and processes that protects against intrusions that includes at least:\n\nProtecting your network by establishing firewalls\n\nUpdating virus protections software/security programs\n\nEncrypting sensitive business and client data\n\nUsing multi factor authentication, whenever available\n\nBacking up critical data and applications regularly\n\nMaking sure each employee has unique passwords and/or accounts to ensure accountability for their actions\n\nFor more on how home builders can protect their businesses’ and clients’ data, visit the Data Privacy and Cybersecurity page on nahb.org.\n\nLatest from NAHBNow\n\nView all\n\nLatest Economic News"}
