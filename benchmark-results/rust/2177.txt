Adequate training is required to properly identify and control hazards to keep the environment safe for those around.\n\n10 Electrical Safety Rules\n\nWhether you’re at work or home, both settings operate on electricity. Here are 10 electrical safety tips to help you avoid electrical hazards:\n\n1. Keep electrical equipment away from water\n\nKeep water and other liquids at least 5 feet away from electrical equipment and sources of electricity. It is recommended to install Ground Fault Circuit Interrupters (GFCIs) which help prevent electrical incidents like electrical shock, ground faults, fires, overheating, and the destruction of wire insulation.\n\n2. Unplug equipment safely\n\nWhen disconnecting electrical equipment, gently pull it by the plug instead of jerking the electrical cord. Pulling by the cord can damage the electrical cable and cause a shock.\n\n3. Install properly and tidy electrical cords\n\nGood electrical installation consists of fixed electrical equipment and electrical cords out of the way (and out of reach of pets that can chew on them).\n\n4. Familiarize yourself with your switchboard\n\nA switchboard is an electrical safety device that protects and isolates the operator from electrical current. It controls the flow of electricity by dividing, distributing, and metering electrical power to different parts and devices. To ensure best performance and safety, electrical power entering the switchboard must resemble the amount of power it distributes. Otherwise, it can cause overheating and lead to electrical fire.\n\nCreate your own Electrical Inspection checklist\n\nBuild from scratch or choose from our collection of free, ready-to-download, and customizable templates.\n\n5. Look out for electrical lines\n\nBe aware of power lines before climbing a tree or a ladder, and especially when working at height.\n\n6. Childproof your outlets\n\nUse outlet covers when there are children around to avoid electrocution.\n\n7. Investigate flickering lights\n\nHave flickering lights checked and fixed, as it can be caused by loose connections somewhere or the bulb itself needs to be secured or replaced.\n\n8. Install warning signs\n\nUse clear visible signs when there’s high voltage in the vicinity that people need to be warned about.\n\n9. Don’t DIY\n\nInstead of fixing issues, most DIYs can cause further damage. Should any electrical issues arise, it is always best to have an expert handle it.\n\n10. Call for help\n\nIn case of an emergency, don’t hesitate to call the emergency services who will talk you through how to safely assist in the situation until help arrives.\n\nImprove your EHS Management\n\nCultivate a safe working environment and streamline compliance with our EHS solutions.\n\nSafetyCulture Marketplace: Your One-Stop Work Gear & Equipment Shop\n\nImprove safety in your workplace with SafetyCulture Marketplace as your one-stop shop for all work gear and equipment needs.
