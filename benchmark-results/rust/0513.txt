{"title":"DASH Eating Plan","author":null,"date":"2025-01-03T14:00:00+00:00","main_content":"DASH (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension) is a flexible and balanced eating plan that helps create a heart-healthy eating style for life. It was named “Best Heart-Healthy Diet” and “Best Diet for High Blood Pressure” by U.S. News & World Report in 2025.\n\nDescription of the DASH Eating Plan\n\nThe DASH eating plan requires no special foods and instead provides daily and weekly nutritional goals. This plan recommends:\nEating vegetables, fruits, and whole grains\nIncluding fat-free or low-fat dairy products, fish, poultry, beans, nuts, and vegetable oils\nLimiting foods that are high in saturated fat, such as fatty meats, full-fat dairy products, and tropical oils such as coconut, palm kernel, and palm oils\nLimiting sugar-sweetened beverages and sweets\n\nBased on these recommendations, the following table shows examples of daily and weekly servings that meet DASH eating plan targets for a 2,000-calorie-a-day diet.\n\nFood Group Daily Servings\nGrains 6–8\nMeats, poultry, and fish 6 or less\nVegetables 4–5\nFruit 4–5\nLow-fat or fat-free dairy products 2–3\nFats and oils 2–3\nSodium 2,300 mg*\nWeekly Servings\nNuts, seeds, dry beans, and peas 4–5\nSweets 5 or less\n\n*1,500 milligrams (mg)\nsodium\n lowers blood pressure even further than 2,300 mg sodium daily.\n\nWhen following the DASH eating plan, it is important to choose foods that are:\nLow in saturated and trans fats\nRich in potassium, calcium, magnesium, fiber, and protein\nLower in sodium\n\nThe number of servings you should have each day depends on your daily calorie needs.
