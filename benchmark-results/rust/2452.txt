The official fix is to replace the loose bolts and reinstall the transmissions. It’s a complicated repair for what should have been a simple tightening job.\n\nIn the video comments, many people confirmed they’ve seen the same issue. One person who works at a Chevy dealer said their coworker dealt with the exact same problem about a month ago. Another commenter explained that assembly line workers aren’t to blame because the bolts are tightened by automated equipment calibrated by engineers, meaning the problem is likely with the machinery itself. This issue isn’t the only dealership warranty work gone wrong that owners have faced recently.\n\nThe repair process is causing major problems for owners. On Reddit, one person said their 2025 Traverse Z71 with less than 3,000 miles had the same leak from loose bolts. The replacement bolts are reportedly on national backorder, making repairs take months.\n\nOne owner shared that their vehicle has been in the shop for almost five months waiting for parts.
