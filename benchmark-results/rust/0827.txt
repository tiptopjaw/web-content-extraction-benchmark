nutrition gaps you’d like to address. \n\nHere’s what to consider when selecting the best multivitamin for you.\n\nYour Body’s Needs\n\nBefore purchasing multivitamins, we recommend that you talk with your doctor or other healthcare professional. If possible and/or necessary, now may be a good time to get bloodwork done so you can find a multivitamin formula best suited to your needs. It’s still best to prioritize getting your recommended daily values of nutrients from whole foods, but a multivitamin may help fill gaps in your diet.\n\nForm\n\nMultivitamins come in a variety of forms—such as gummies, capsules, tablets, powders, and even liquids—and it’s important to choose the type that fits best with your lifestyle and preferences. Some people find gummies easier to take, while others prefer traditional capsules or tablets for their convenience and higher nutrient content. Powders and liquids can also be good options if swallowing pills is difficult. The key is to choose a multivitamin in a form you’ll take consistently, since daily use will provide you with the best results.\n\nCost\n\nMultivitamins are typically taken every day, and can be pricey, so you may want to consider your budget when selecting your multivitamin. Cleaner formulations, meaning those without artificial sweeteners or additives, may cost more because of their higher quality and ingredient sourcing.\n\nThird-Party Testing\n\nThird-party testing independently verifies the safety, purity, and label accuracy of multivitamins since the FDA doesn’t regulate supplements. This process helps protect you from mislabeling, contamination, and banned substances, building trust and ensuring you can use products with confidence. Independent testing is essential for confirming product quality and safeguarding your health. Third-party certifications are especially crucial if you’re in the market for a fat burner supplement or testosterone booster, for instance.\n\nBenefits of Multivitamins\n\nTaking a multivitamin daily can support your overall health by filling nutritional gaps and promoting key functions in your body.\nStrong bones: Nutrients like vitamin D and calcium in multivitamins can help maintain bone density and may reduce the risk of osteoporosis\n\nImmune health: Vitamins C, D, and zinc support the immune system ’s ability to fight off infections\n\nCognitive health: B vitamins, particularly B6, B12, and folate, play a role in supporting memory and brain function as we age\n\nOverall energy: Iron and B vitamins help convert food into energy, which may reduce fatigue and improve daily stamina.\n\nHow to Take Multivitamins\n\nWhen taking a multivitamin, it’s important to follow the dosage instructions on the product label or the guidance provided by your doctor. Taking higher-than-recommended amounts of certain vitamins and minerals can lead to side effects or even health risks over time. Consistency is key, so choose a time of day that works best for you and make it part of your routine.\n\nIt also helps to know whether the vitamins in your supplement are water- or fat-soluble. \n\nWater-soluble vitamins like vitamin C and the B-complex family, dissolve in water and are best absorbed on an empty stomach, though they need to be replenished daily since the body does not store them. \n\nFat-soluble vitamins, such as A, D, E, and K, are absorbed more effectively when taken with a meal that contains some healthy fat. Pairing your multivitamin with food can therefore improve its overall benefits.\n\nIt’s also important to consider possible medication interactions.
