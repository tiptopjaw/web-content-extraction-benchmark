{"title":"I played some games this year","author":"Juha-Matti Santala","date":"2025-12-20T00:00:00+00:00","main_content":"Dec 20th, 2025\n\nby Juha-Matti Santala\n\nThis was published in categories:\n\ngaming\n\nIt’s been another year of gaming and I wanted to highlight some of my\nfavourites from this year. Games I played this\nyear, not necessarily games published this\nyear. These are not in any kind of order really.\n\nI’m sharing these in the spirit of\nmore human curation over algorithmic recommendations.\n\nNHL 21\n\nThe list may start with a bit of a surprise. I’ve been playing hockey games\never since I learned how to hold a controller or smash a keyboard. Since the\nearly 1990s. It all started with the\nFace Off!, then with EA’s\nNHLPA Hockey '93\nand\nNHL ‘94\non Super Nintendo, then\nNHL 97\non PC that I played for years. At the same time, I played\nOlympic Hockey ‘98\non Nintendo 64 and my mind was blown when\nNHL 2003\non Xbox came out.\n\nAround 2006, I switched to\n2K series\nfor a while until in 2010, I got the demo of NHL 2010 and the skill stick\nexperience changed the way of playing virtual hockey forever.\n\nPeople always talk about these sports games as just annual roster updates and\nthat’s why I’ve usually been updating around 3 years as there usually is\nenough small incremental changes that accumulate in a few years so that the\nnew ones feel fresh and usually better.\n\nI’ve been sticking with NHL 21 for over 5 years already and I’m still enjoying\nit.\n\n“Enjoying it” might be an understatement. Every year since its release, it’s\nbeen my most played game on Playstation by quite a distance. In 2023, I played\n134 hours of it. In 2024, I played 124 hours. This year? 168 hours.\n\nI’ve been thinking about maybe updating next year to get some of the newer\nteams and rosters but I’m in year 12 or something of a very nice GM run and it\nwould be a shame to stop that now.\n\nIf I were made to choose only one game I could play from here on, I’d pick\nthis one. I’ll never get bored of it.\n\nStardew Valley\n\nConcernedApe’s masterpiece\nStardew Valley\nisn’t a new game either. I purchased it for my birthday in 2017, a year after\nits launch and have racked way over 1000 hours in Steam — and that’s only\ncounting the hours I’ve played when online so the real number is even higher.\n\nIn the world of microtransactions, shutting down servers, DLCs and all the\nother crap, Stardew Valley is what I wish more games would aspire to be. It’s\nbeen almost 10 years since the launch and it still receives updates. Not just\nbug fixes and stuff but actual story content. The team is working on the\nseventh update, 1.7., that’s been rumored to maybe arrive for the 10 year\nanniversary.\n\nIt’s not just the official updates either. The modding community is wonderful\nand there are so many great, very deep content mods that brings new fresh life\nto the game just when you thought you’re getting bored of it.\n\nIf you’re not familiar with it, Stardew Valley is a cozy farming game with\nelements of relationships with villagers, mining, fishing, combat and making a\nshit ton of money by selling wine in a town with 20 or so adults. Who’s buying\nand drinking all this wine? Millions worth of it, every month.\n\nBlue Prince\n\nI have a love-hate relationship with puzzle games. I mean, I love them and if\nit was up to me, I could spend hours every day solving puzzles. But I’m also\nvery bad at them and they mess up my sleep with horrible nightmares really\neasily so I need to ration my intake.\n\nThis year, I gave\nBlue Prince\ntwo opportunities.\n\nBlue Prince is a mansion drafting puzzle rogue-lite. Every day, you wake up in\nthe courtyard of a mansion and try to make your way through it to solve its\nmysteries. Whenever you open a door to a new room, you get to draft a new room\nfrom a selection of (usually) 3 different rooms. There are a lot of mysteries\nand I have barely scratched the surface so I don’t know what they are yet.\n\nI first got it in the spring when it came out and internet was raving about\nit. Most likely after watching this\nGame Maker’s Toolkit video on the game. I played it and after a ton of attempts, I finally got through to one of\nthe mysteries of the game — around day 70 or so. Only to find out, it was not\na permanent upgrade but something that had to be solved EVERY TIME. That broke\nme and I abandoned the game.\n\nIn November, I got back to it and equipped with better understanding of the\ngame, started a new fresh run and made quite a lot of progress in just a\nfraction of the days spent. I still haven’t gotten back to the point where I\nwas in the spring but I have a bit more permanent upgrades and goodies\nunlocked so I’ve been enjoying it a bit more this time.\n\nCyberpunk 2077\n\nThanks to this summer’s\nMac release, I finally got to experience\nCyberpunk 2077\nproperly. I had borrowed it from the library for PS4 before but at the first\nfight scene, it turned into a slideshow and I couldn’t ever get through that\nforced moment.\n\nI’ve been wanting to play this game for so long and it’s been great. I’m a big\nfan of cyberpunk genre and I feel the game has captured many aspects of it\nbrilliantly. I love roaming the city, interacting with the characters and\nhacking away.\n\nAt the same time, I feel like it’s not gonna be a game that I’ll return over\nand over again in the future. It’s more of a one-off (or two, or three off)\nexperience and that’s fine too.\n\nMarvel’s Spider-Man\n\nI might be a bit late to the party with this one but I finally played\nMarvel’s Spider-Man\nthat’s been highly praised especially for its web-slinging movement mechanics.\nAnd yes, they are fun. But also, there’s a lot of it — too much to my taste.\nThe first few hours it’s so much fun. After 12 hours, it gets boring because\nall the missions are spread out in a way that you need to cross half of New\nYork to get to the location.\n\nDespite that criticism, the game is great. Definitely worth all the praise\nit’s gotten. The story is your run of the mill Spider-Man story but it does\nits job well. Occasionally, you get to play as MJ and while usually these\ntypes of gimmicks are poorly done in games, I actually enjoyed these as well.\nWhile Spider-Man flies around the city and kicks and punches his way through\nthe bad guys, MJ takes a more stealthy approach as a good journalist does.\n\nHoop Land\n\nI don’t play a lot of games on mobile but there are a couple of games I’ve\nbeen enjoying on my iPad that I want to share. After years of enjoying\nRetro Bowl, the game became bit too repetitive and I was looking for something new. I\nran into\nHoop Land, a basketball game with surprising depth.\n\nRetro Bowl’s simple controls and mechanics made it a perfect game to play on a\ntiny breaks but Hoop Land essentially requires a controller so it has filled a\ndifferent type of void. But when you sit down to play, oh boy is it fun. It\nhas a bit of a learning curve and in the beginning, I got smacked in the head\nevery game but eventually started to get the ball into the basket.\n\nFor those moments when all I have with me is the iPad and a controller, it’s\nreal good entertainment where I can put hours into.\n\nTatami\n\nTatami\nis a lovely puzzle game by Kaylee Calderolla. The mechanics are simple: you\nneed to group tiles on a grid into rectangles based on the rules imposed by\neach level. The levels get more difficult as time goes on.\n\nAs it’s a puzzle game that I need to ration to maintain a healthy-ish sleep\ncycle, I’m limiting myself to maximum couple of levels a day and early in the\nmorning. I’m currently at level 231 and the puzzles are starting to get real\ndifficult here.\n\nThe first 200 levels are free but it’s a single purchase game that’s built\nwith such care for the users that I recommend purchasing it right away.\n\nPokémon TCG\n\nMoving (partly) away from the digital realm, my number one game this year was\nPokémon TCG. I played a ton both in-person as well as digitally. One of the\nhighlights of the year was running a\nScarlet & Violet Progression Series\nthat we started in September and finished just before Christmas.\n\nI played in a bunch of Leagues and Prereleases, even won a tiny GLC tournament\nand continued growing my collection of Eeveelutions whenever I could pick up\nnew cards I’m still missing.\n\nI also spent a good chunk of timing building my own tools to manage my card\ncollection (moved away from a service that provided no export and started to\npaywall and enshittify aggressively), built my own card API (when one I had\nused for years started to have issues), built a bunch of tooling to manage the\nprogression series (a public website, a card pool viewer and I’m mid-way\nbuilding a platform where one could manage a complete progression series with\nopening packs, building decks and recording scores. None of that will likely\never be publicly available because I don’t want their lawyers on my ass but\nI’m self-hosting for myself and the growing toolkit has been wonderful so far.\n\nMagic the Gathering\n\nI have been following Magic the Gathering for years but never really got into\nit to play. Mainly because my money already goes to Pokémon TCG and can’t\nafford two TCG hobbies simultaneously.\n\nThen last year, they published\nBloomburrow\nand I was sold. This year, I actually started playing. Not a ton but more and\nmore as the year progressed. I built my own\nBattle Box\nfor easy pick-and-play at the pub, I built a\nDandân, Forgetful Fish\ndeck for similar type of game play.\n\nAnd when a friend asked if I’d like to join their group of casual Commander, I\npurchased my first Commander pre-con deck\nFamily Matters\nand we started playing regularly.\n\nParks\n\nI didn’t get to play as much\nParks\nas I would have wanted this year but it has slowly climbed up the ranks of my\nfavourite board games. It has such nice aesthetics, great gameplay and it’s\nthe type of game that provides depth but is still kind to beginners and as\nsuch, can be a great gateway\ndrug game to get new people into the\nhobby.\n\nIn Parks, you and your friends are hiking at national parks, gathering\nresources, taking photos and experiencing the great wild nature.\n\nNHL 94/25 romhack\n\nWhile NHL 21 has been my main way of enjoying virtual hockey, Adam Catalyst’s\nNHL25 romhack for NHL ‘94\nhas been a delight. NHL ‘94 is one of the most iconic hockey games ever and\none I played a lot as a kid. So whenever there’s a way to return to that\niconic experience with something new and fresh, I’m all for it.\n\nThis romhack brings modern teams and rosters, alongside with gameplay tweaks\nand graphics refinements. It’s a perfect nostalgia trip while still getting to\nenjoy all the benefits of the most recent roster updates.\n\nIt’s funny that if I want to play with Seattle Kraken or Utah Mammoth, I need\nto abandon my modern PS4 experience and play the game from over 30 years ago.\n\nPokeRogue\n\nPokéRogue, a\nfan-made rogue-lite Pokémon game continues to entertain me. I have an\noffline-version on my Steam Deck that I haven’t updated since I created it (I\nshould check if my documented workflow still works).\n\nPokéRogue is a game where you have 10 points to use to pick your starting\nparty: the better the Pokémon, more expensive it is to add. Then you fight\nrandomly generated opponents, catch wild monsters to add them to your party\nand as new starting Pokémon and try to get through the game.\n\nIt’s a lot of fun!\n\nFactorio\n\nFactorio is\none of those games that I had to put on a shelf in April when I got a job. I\ncouldn’t afford booting it up, blinking my eyes and noticing it’s 5 in the\nmorning. I’m kind of looking forward to the Christmas holidays and dedicating\na few days to just getting lost in the world of Factorio.\n\nEvery time we talk about video games with friends, Factorio comes up. It has a\ndevious lack of cycles, if that makes any sense. There’s never a “now is a\ngood moment to stop playing” moment. You just keep on going and going and\ngoing, solving the next optimisation puzzle. Until you notice you haven’t\neaten or slept in ages.\n\nI always play it on a safe mode, ie. turn off the aggressive creatures. The\ngame is complicated enough to try to solve without the constant worry of\nsomeone wrecking my base.\n\nIn Factorio, you’re stranded on a strange planet and need to make the best out\nof the bad situation. You start by collecting some copper and iron ore and\ncoal by hand and little by little build a massive automated factory with the\naim to conquer space.\n\nI’d love to recommend the game to everyone. If you care about your sleep\nthough, maybe give it a pass 😅.\n\nIf something above resonated with you, let's start a discussion about it!
