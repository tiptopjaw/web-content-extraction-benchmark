{"title":"Writing Concisely — Center for Arts & Language","author":null,"date":null,"main_content":"Writing Concisely — the Paramedic Method\n\nWordy writing may be a necessary step in discovering your ideas, but writers often revise their writing to eliminate excess in favor of vigorous and engaging text. To achieve it, ask yourself what is essential and allow every word to speak for itself. \n\nThe Paramedic Method, developed by Richard Lanham ( Revising Prose ), is an effective method for eliminating unnecessary words. It requires methodical attention at first, but once you start to recognize patterns, writing and editing for concision will come naturally. Here’s how to do it:\n\nExample sentence: The point I wish to make is that perception is the process of extracting information from stimulation emanating from the objects, places, and events in the world around us.\n\n1. Eliminate slow wind-ups \n\nThe point I wish to make is that perception is the process of extracting information from stimulation emanating from the objects, places, and events in the world around us.\n\n2. Eliminate clichés (like “in a nutshell” or “better late than never”)\n\nPerception is the process of extracting information from stimulation emanating from the objects, places, and events in the world around us.\n\n3. Find all prepositions and “to be” verbs in all forms; create a line break at each, making a list of phrases\n\nPerception\nis the process\nof extracting information\nfrom stimulation emanating\nfrom objects, places, and events.\n\n4. Identify the possible “actions” and transform them into single verbs  \n\nPerception\nis the process   >> Process\nof extracting information >> Extract, Inform\nfrom stimulation emanating >> Stimulate, Emanate\nfrom objects, places, and events.\n\n5. Identify the subject of the sentence and choose the clearest action verb for that subject to take on.\n\nSubject: perception                        Verb: extract\n\n6. Bring the subject and verb together; eliminate other verbs and excess prepositional phrases\n\nPerception is the process of extracts ing information from stimulation emanating from objects, places, and events.\n\nConcise revision: Perception extracts information from objects, places, and events."}
