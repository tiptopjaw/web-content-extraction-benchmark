{"title":"Announcing the 2025 AI &#038; RISC-V Gemini Credit Recipients","author":"abringaze","date":"2025-12-18T16:22:08+00:00","main_content":"And the winners are…\n\nIn September we sent out a call for proposals, looking for researchers and academics that would leverage AI to speed up the RISC-V software ecosystem. Grant recipients would receive Gemini compute credits up to $50k USD and the opportunity to collaborate with the RISC-V Software Ecosystem Project (RISE) and RISC-V International on their research to drive positive impact for the ecosystem. \n\nAfter careful consideration and review of all applicants by a panel of experts at RISC-V International, we’re excited to announce a series of grant recipients. We strongly believe each will help further the AI ecosystem in a new and compelling way, helping AI and RISC-V grow in one or all of the following areas:\n\nInnovative Research: Create new AI-driven methods to speed up software porting, from new algorithms and fine-tuned models to agentic approaches.\n\nEssential Tooling: Build generalizable tools and infrastructure like test suite generators, simulators, or formal verification systems that accelerate the RISC-V porting process.\n\nCritical Progress: Directly port key software, especially for server use cases, to make a significant, immediate impact on the RISC-V ecosystem.\n\nWithout further ado, let’s congratulate our winners:\n\nAli Jannesari (Iowa State University)\n\nLeonidas Kosmidis (Barcelona Supercomputing Center)\n\nAbdulrahman Mahmoud (Mohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial Intelligence)\n\nDaniel Mueller-Gritschneder (TU Wien)\n\nSophia Shao and Alvin Cheung (University of California, Berkeley)\n\nWe are proud to support these researchers as they help unlock new possibilities for the RISC-V ecosystem and AI. Hurrah!"}
