It can help you reach more people and scale your existing coaching, consulting, or expert business while requiring less time than your one-on-one services. However, you need to know how to price your course to create one that is both profitable and sustainable.\n\nLet’s start with an overview of different price points: \n\nA mini course, a simple video training series with about 4-10 videos, each under 15 minutes, can be sold for anywhere from $47 to about $147.\n\nA shorter online course with audio/videos and PDF’s typically sells for anywhere from $197 to $497. Pricing online courses doesn’t have to be hard, but you do want to know what the steps involved are. \n\nYour course price is determined by factors such as:\n\nThe results you promise \n\nWho your target audience is \n\nYour experience/background \n\nYour course features\n\nYour costs  \n\nHere are the steps to pricing your course: \n\n1. Look at the results you offer \n\nTo set your course price, think about the results you offer. Because if your course can promise results that people really want, price and format are secondary. \n\nThat said, when you create your first course, you likely don’t have THAT many client testimonials (unless your coaching business already has a lot of them). So if you’re in doubt about your pricing, start with a lower price. You can always raise your prices after you’ve sold your course the first time around.\n\nIf you create a group coaching program, your price could be somewhere between $997 to $1,997. And once you have a few students, you can raise your rates to $2,997 and more.\n\nHowever, if you decide to start with a self-study course without any group coaching, I recommend starting with a course that costs between $197 to $497.\n\nThis type of course is easier to create and less overwhelming. So learn how to sell your course at a lower price and then move on to a higher priced “A to Z” course that you can price at $1,000 to $3,000. \n\nThat’s what I did, too. I first created my “First Steps” course Your First Paying Clients, which teaches people how to get their first few clients.\n\nI then moved on to selling my Employee to Entrepreneur course, which is a course that teaches everything you need to know about starting a coaching business. \n\nUltimately, if you find that it’s difficult to set a price, just pick one and focus on creating and selling your course. Understand what factors determine your price\n\nThere are factors that determine how much you can charge for your course. Your audience, experience, and course features all impact the price of your course.\n\nHere’s what I mean:\n\nYour audience \n\nFirst, you need to know your audience. \n\nWho are you targeting with your course? And why are you targeting them? Is your course the best fit for that specific audience? \n\nYou will need to know your audience to set a price.\n\nFor example, someone who is early in their career and is looking for a career course will typically have less money to spend than someone who is further ahead. \n\nAsk yourself: \n\nIs your audience willing and able to pay for your course? And how much are they willing to pay for it? \n\nLook at courses with similar audiences to get an idea. \n\nYour experience and background\n\nThis part can include your certifications, your formal education, personal experiences you’ve had or that you’ve helped others with solving this problem, and of course, testimonials from those you’ve helped.\n\nIf you don’t have any testimonials right now, you can offer a few free one-time sessions to help people get results and get that proof you need. You definitely WILL need some proof to show people you know what you’re talking about and to build trust. \n\nYour course features \n\nWhat do people get when they enroll in your course? The features themselves aren’t what make your course valuable, but you do need to know what your course will look like to set a price. \n\nThink through if you’re going to offer video, audio, and written material (PDF’s and ebooks) and how you’ll present your course. \n\nAlso consider: \n\nHow long will your course be? This depends on how much content you need to help your clients. But don’t confuse value with length. In fact, a more concise course will often help your students much better than a long course with everything crammed into it because a concise course is easier to implement. \n\nHow will you deliver your course? You can use videos, audio, calls, and written content to deliver your course. But be clear on this when you price your course because a PDF or ebook won’t sell for as much as a video course. \n\nWhat support are you going to offer to your clients? The support you offer can significantly increase the value, and price, of your course. So think through how you support your clients with group coaching calls and feedback. \n\n3. Take your costs into account\n\nWhen you price your course, you do need to take your other costs into account. How much money will you ultimately keep? \n\nThis isn’t as important when you’re building your first course. After all, you have other things to focus on like creating it, figuring out how to sell your course, and delivering it.\n\nBut once you’ve sold your course successfully, it might be time to set a more strategic price for it. \n\nTo do so, calculate your bottom line, set a budget for your marketing, and track your income/expenses. That way, you’ll know if your pricing is on par with your marketing expenses (for instance, if you run ads and are unsure about what your ads should cost). \n\nBut there’s one thing you need to know about course pricing… You can increase the value of your course – and not care too much about what price you set!\n\nHow to increase the value of your course \n\nHere’s the thing: Selling a course isn’t about the price at all. \n\nI used to be scared of charging the price that I really wanted to the point that I started to resent it. As a result, I stayed stuck in charging the same price for years. \n\nBut once I realized that selling a course has little to do with the price, I began to set whatever price felt good to me. And no matter the price, my programs always sell. \n\nIt can be easy to look around and think, “people sell courses for $49, so I guess I have to do the same.” You don’t. \n\nThose $49 courses offer a completely different experience and results than higher-priced courses. And there are customers for both types of offers.\n\nSure, your price depends on the type of course you sell. If it’s a simple PDF or a mini-course, you need to adjust your price accordingly.\n\nBut don’t assume you have to set a low price to sell it. \n\nAs research shows, people value higher-priced products more than lower-priced products because they associate a high price with quality. \n\nPsychologist Robert Cialdini highlights a telling example in his book Influence. When a shop wanted to put items on sale but accidentally increased the prices, they sold much faster than they had before. \n\nIn other words, premium pricing can help with selling your product. \n\nThe benefits of charging a higher price for your course \n\nBy charging a premium price, you: \n\nBuild a more profitable business: The price you charge massively impacts how much money you take home. If you want to build a sustainable coaching business, your profit margins need to be as high as possible. \n\nCan grow faster: By asking for a higher price, you can spend more on marketing your course…and ultimately, grow your business faster. \n\nOffer better results: When you don’t have to drive as many online course sales to make a good profit, you can spend more of your time on improving your course.\n\nAttract the right clients: The more people pay for your course, the more motivated they tend to be. After all, they see it as far more valuable than lower priced courses. \n\nUltimately, selling a premium price course takes the same effort as selling a low priced course, so why not set a higher price? \n\nThat said, especially in the beginning when you don’t have as many testimonials or “proof” that your course offers tons of value, you need to increase the perceived value of your course to ask for a higher price. \n\n“Perceived value” is the value people assign to your course based on how likely they think it is that your course will help them. \n\nThe best steps to increase your course’s perceived value are:\n\nDiscover the top 3 reasons most courses fail\n\n(plus how to fix them so you succeed)\n\n1.
