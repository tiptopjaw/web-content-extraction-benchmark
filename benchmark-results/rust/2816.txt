{"title":"Backup Strategy","author":"Jeff Kaufman","date":null,"main_content":"Backup Strategy January 22nd, 2019\nBackup Strategy tech\n\nAfter reading a post from someone who nearly lost all their data to a\njoint NAS and\nexternal hard drive failure I decided to think through my data\ndurability.\n\nMost of my stuff lives in the cloud: email on Gmail, docs in Google\nDocs, photos in Google Photos, data in Google Drive. I trust Google a\nlot for this sort of thing, both given their public reputation and as\nan employee. I think Google is extremely unlikely to lose or corrupt\nmy data.\n\nThe next biggest place where I have things is on the server that runs\njefftk.com. I back this up to my laptop with a cronjob that\nlooks like:\n\nrsync -t -l -r -c ps:jtk/ www-jefftk-com/ --delete-after\nrsync -t -l -r -c ps:tc/ www-trycontra-com/ --delete-after\nrsync -t -l -r -c ps:fr/ www-freeraisins-com/ --delete-after\n...\nrsync -t -l -r -c ps:kf/ www-kingfisherband-com/ --delete-after\nrsync -t -l -r -c ps: ps-rest/ --delete-after\nrsync -t -l -r -c ps:/etc/ ps-etc/ --delete-after\n\nThis copies each of my websites, the rest of my home directory, and\nthe contents of /etc. It's set to run once a day, if my\nlaptop is on then, which it is more often than not. It copies them\ninto a subdirectory of ~/Google Drive, which means it then\nall gets synced to Drive.\n\nI also have a cronjob set up to run twice a month that backs up the\ncomments on my blog posts. It pulls out the\n/wsgi/json-comments/ urls from my blog posts, fetches them\nall, and saves them. When Google Plus gets turned off I'm planning to\nconfigure my comment system to serve those comments from my backup.\n\nFinally, code lives on\nGitHub plus local checkouts on my server, laptop, or both.\n\nEverything on my laptop is temporary, and anything important is under\n~/Google Drive. All my dotfiles, including my ~/.full_history,\nare symlinks to into ~/Google Drive.\n\nThe main weakness in this setup is its dependency on Drive.\nSpecifically:\n\nFor the portion of Google Drive synced to my laptop, if I\naccidentally deleted or changed things locally that could be synced\nup. If I noticed right away there's the Drive Trash which\nkeeps things for 30 days, or I could contact support. Something that\ncorrupted files in a subtle way could be pretty bad.\n\nVery occasionally people have lost access to their Google\naccounts. For example, by forgetting\nyour password or getting\nmistaken for a fake account. If this happened to me it would be a\ndisaster: so much of what I use is a Google service.\n\nI think both of these are too unlikely to be worth mitigating though.\nThey could happen, but they're much less likely than the sort of\nfailure (~1%/year) that you're trying to avoid by backing things up.\n\nUpdate 2019-01-26: the day after posting this I spilled water\non my laptop, but it was actually fine.\n\nComment via: facebook, substack"}
