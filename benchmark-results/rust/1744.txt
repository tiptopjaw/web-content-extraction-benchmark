The survey was conducted by online market research company Slice MR.\n\nRespondents were asked to name their insurer and then grade it in a number of categories, including: \n\nCustomer satisfaction\n\nClaims satisfaction\n\nPrice/affordability\n\nEase of service \n\nPolicy offerings\n\nDigital experience\n\nWe then asked respondents to provide a yes or no response to indicate their agreement with the following statements:\n\nI plan to keep my coverage with my current insurance company\n\nI would recommend my insurance company to others\n\nI trust my insurance company.\n\nThe editors compiled the survey results and then selected – based on the number of survey responses – the top companies for further evaluation.\n\nFor each category, the editors then gathered rankings and ratings from respected third parties.\n\nThe editors collected AM Best data, which measure financial strength, and National Association of Insurance Commissioners’ complaint data, which ranks a company by the number of customer complaints it receives. The Insure.com team identified the NAIC codes of each underwriting company for each carrier and calculated a weighted average complaint index based on annual written premiums. The associated NAIC complaint index score was used in the calculations. \n\nThey collected insurance rate data from Quadrant Information Services (for auto and home insurance), Compulife (for life insurance) and the public health marketplace (for health insurance).\n\nFor health insurance, the editors also collected data from the National Committee for Quality Assurance (NCQA), which evaluates and rates health plans.\n\nWith the help of Prof. David Marlett, Ph.D., managing director of the Brantley Risk and Insurance Center at Appalachian State University, the editors created a rating system to determine which insurance companies were best in each sector.\n\nFor auto and home insurers, we took the following and gave each a weight:\n\nSurvey: 40% of the total score (10% customer satisfaction, 10% recommended, 10% renewal rate and 10% claims handling)\n\nAM Best: 25% of total score\n\nAnnual premium: 20% of total score\n\nNAIC: 15% of total score \n\nFor life insurers, we took the following and gave each a weight:\n\nSurvey: 60% of the total score (10% customer satisfaction, 10% ease of service, 10% policy offerings, 10% recommended, 10% policy retention and 10% trust)\n\nAM Best: 25% of total score\n\nNAIC: 15% of total score\n\nFor health insurers, we took the following and gave each a weight:\n\nSurvey: 60% of the total score (10% customer satisfaction, 10% recommended, 10% policy retention, 10% trust, and 20% affordability)\n\nNCQA: 25% of total score\n\nNAIC: 15% of total score\n\nIn all categories, insurers were awarded between half a star and 5 stars.
