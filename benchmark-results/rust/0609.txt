{"title":"OMCP® Digital Marketing Certification Requirements","author":null,"date":"2014-01-29T06:52:51+00:00","main_content":"The OMCP® digital marketing certification is awarded to professionals who have verified experience, extensive training, and have demonstrated knowledge of digital marketing best practices across multiple digital marketing disciplines. Over 900 universities and training institutes teach to OMCP standards worldwide. Thousands of leading companies prefer to hire professionals with OMCA and OMCP credentials. Is it time for you to become a certified marketing professional?\n\nBenefits of OMCP certification\n\nIndividuals  who earn the OMCP certification:\n\nStand out  to employers and clients as having verified digital marketing skills, education, and experience\n\nAre in demand as specialists, leaders, and accredited instructors\n\nEarn  between 16-26% more than non-certified digital marketers\n\nOperate more effectively  as a marketing professional, sharing common language and generally accepted practices across multiple disciplines and in at least two specialty disciplines\n\nTeams  and agencies that hire and train to OMCA and OMCP digital marketing standards  lead the industry  in marketing performance.\n\nThree steps to OMCP certification:\n\n1. Ensure you have the required experience and education\n\nThere is no prerequisite to take OMCP exams, but if you are pursuing OMCP certification, ensure you have minimum experience, and education. OMCP certification requires:\nPassing scores on the OMCP exams AND any one of the following:\n5,000 hours of experience Experience for OMCP Valid experience must be documentable hours performing marketing work, whether paid or not. Marketing work is defined as work that requires knowledge, application, or communication of marketing principles. This can include any marketing job, traditional marketing, e.g., print, copywriting, advertising, selling marketing services, or providing some subset of marketing services to a marketing team or to paying clients.\n\nOR 2,000 hours experience plus a post-secondary degree\n\nOR 1,000 hours experience plus completion of an approved digital marketing course within six months of application\n\nIf you meet the qualifications, open a free account with OMCP where you’ll set your goals for certification, track your status, and upload proof of completion for your certification evaluation. Based on your choices, you’ll receive a confirmation with specific requirements, tips and encouragement from hiring managers, and messages from certified marketers who are currently experiencing the benefits of OMCP certification earned years ago.\n\n2. Pass your OMCP exams\n\nAn OMCP must pass two specialty exams and one broad-spectrum digital marketing exam. The OMCP Exam is proctored online, so there’s no need to travel or wait.\n\nOnce you have completed your OMCP exams within 90 days, upload your credentials for final approval for certification.\n\n3. Request your certification review\n\nTo apply for certification status, upload your proof, and after a successful review by OMCP staff, get a confirmed certification status.\n\nFrequently-Asked Questions about OMCP Certification:\n\nHow and where do I take the exams?\n\nThe OMCP Exams are proctored online, so there’s no need to travel. Exams can be scheduled to be taken at any time and from anywhere the exam taker has a reliable internet connection.\n\nTo sit for your exams, you must have a web camera and a microphone so you and your proctor may communicate. Resources of any kind are not allowed during the exam. This includes, books, notes, access to online information, texts, chats,  or phone calls, or having another person in the room while the exam is happening.\n\nHow do I prepare for the OMCP exam?\n\nPlanning ahead for your exam will help ensure that you have the best chance to be successful and that the exam process works smoothly for you.\n\nPreparation for the OMCP exam must include a combination of practical experience in online marketing coupled with continuing education in recent accepted practices.  No course or published book will completely prepare a candidate to pass the OMCP exam.  The minimum recommended preparation for the OMCP exam includes 1,000 hours of online marketing experience that includes conceptual understanding of at least six online marketing disciplines, coupled with 64 didactic hours in  OMCP-Approved Courses  covering two specific disciplines.  Candidates with 3-5 years of practical discipline-specific implementation experience may need less education, and candidates with more education may not need as much practical experience.  In most cases, a balanced combination of experience and up-to-date education is essential.\n\nThe OMCP Exam structure comprises base-knowledge questions which test conceptual knowledge across multiple online marketing disciplines, plus two additional sections that focus on generally accepted practices in specific core disciplines.\n\nThe following online marketing disciplines are supported by the most recent OMCP Role Delineation Study, are considered core to OMCP certification, and determine the content of the OMCP exam sections:\nContent Marketing\n\nConversion Rate Optimization\n\nEmail Marketing (included within OMCA and OMCP Base exams only)\n\nMobile Marketing (included within OMCA and OMCP Base exams only)\n\nPaid Search (PPC)\n\nSearch Engine Optimization (SEO)\n\nSocial Media Marketing\n\nDigital Analytics\n\nWhat is the cost of the OMCP exams?\n\nThe OMCP exam fee is US $395 for all three parts, payable upon registration. The fee covers online proctoring and the exam delivery systems.  In the event that a retake of any part of the exam is necessary, the OMCP base-knowledge exam retake fee is $225 and the OMCP skills-exam retake fee is $150 per skill-exam. TO meet the exam requirement for OMCP Certification, all three parts of the OMCP Exam must be passed within a 90-day window.  There is currently no cost for application for certification of individuals, though this may change in the future.\n\nHow long will OMCP certification last?\n\nBecause of the rapid changes in digital marketing, OMCP Certifications last two years when renewed with PDUs.  To gain two years of certification, the candidate must use one of the following methods to gain the full two years:\nExtend the certification  for the second year by participating in recognized organizations, events, training programs, and communities to earn  Professional Development Units (PDUs ).\n\nRenew the certificate for the additional year by passing any one OMCP exam.\n\nCurrently, accrual of 100 PDUs during the year is required for extension. Completion of PDU events must have occurred within 12 months before renewal date to be considered for renewal.\n\nTo submit PDUs for OMCP Re-Certification, simply provide the necessary verification for review through your member dashboard.\n\nPassing the OMCP exam is required every two years, at minimum. Extension via PDUs occurs every other year.\n\nEric Kozlik\nDigital Content Manager\nPrice Benowitz, LLP"}
