A lack of resources can lead to unfulfilled expectations, product release delays, and ultimately sunk costs.\n\nThere are over 47 million software developers around the world as of early 2025. That might sound like a lot, but given current demand in the context of wildly fluctuating and expanding business needs, it’s a major challenge for employers to find and retain the right talent and digital solutions.\n\nHowever, don’t rush to fill your open slots using agency services before you truly understand the different types of software development agencies. Whether you need to create enterprise software or mobile applications for e-commerce functionalities, manage big data, or just secure custom web development for other uses, you'll need to be very selective about the development partner you choose.\n\nIn this post, we explore the three different types of agencies, present their pros and cons for startups and other businesses, share an example of each, and offer a quick test to check which type you should engage with. Then we present a list of the top 50 software development agencies to consider.\n\nRather than go straight into vetting different companies, your first step should be to decide which type of agency is the best fit for your needs.\n\nWhat services do software development agencies provide?\n\nThe services that a software development agency will provide depends in large part on the type of agency it is.\n\nFor example, some agencies will only offer software engineering services such as Laravel development and PHP development. They will expect you or someone from your team to handle all strategic product direction as well as project management.\n\nOn the other hand, other agencies will not only provide technical development from the front-end to the backend but also business strategy. Some might offer UX design, UI design, and quality assurance management as well.\n\nIt’s important to consider what you’re looking for now and how your company will likely grow in the future. For example, if a web application might be in your future, ask potential partners about past application development projects, web design experience, whether they work in both iOS and android, and what their UI/UX background is. Can this software development agency grow with the brand to offer new and innovative software solutions?\n\nHow will this software development team assist in building better API security into your products and services? Can they help you achieve business goals safely and securely? If you need bespoke products, do they have case studies that demonstrate their versatility as a custom software development company?\n\nCan your in-house team handle the complexities of developing with user experience in mind? Will you find yourself outsourcing extra support if you don't have a scalable agency on this project?\n\nAnd finally, if you're in fintech, blockchain, healthcare, or other verticals that are heavily regulated, you'll need to ensure that your software development agency can help you meet regulatory requirements and stay compliant with applicable laws.\n\nTop 3 types of software development agencies\n\nEngaging with the wrong kind of agency can stress out your internal resources, hinder your launch timeline, and waste your investment. \n\nThese are the 3 categories of software development agencies:\n\nScalable, fully managed services\n\nBoutique development firm with limited capacity\n\nStaffing agency with no management\n\nLet’s take a look at them in more detail below so you can get a better understanding of where your best path forward might lie. \n\n1. Scalable, fully managed services\n\nFirst up, let’s dive into the type of agency that offers end-to-end development at scale.\n\nWhat this type of software development agency offers\n\nThis category of software development agencies is the most comprehensive and scalable. It typically includes everything required to launch and maintain quality software:\n\nBusiness strategy\n\nProduct management\n\nSoftware engineering\n\nUX/UI\n\nQA management\n\nDevOps\n\nAgile\n\nPros and cons\n\nWhen you choose a fully managed agency, you’re not burdened with coordinating resources and keeping everyone on track. \n\nHowever, you do hand over the reins with this type of agency, so make sure that you choose a company that has some sort of transition services in place, for when you want to manage the team. \n\nExample: DevSquad\n\nFor example, DevSquad enables entrepreneurs and executives to take charge of product management and QA when they are ready.\n\nAs a nearshore software development company, DevSquad offers fully managed software development teams who collaborate remotely from Utah and Latin America.\n\nFocusing primarily on SaaS application development, DevSquad handles product development at every step of the way: from strategic MVP roadmapping to engineering, user-friendly design, DevOps, and QA testing.\n\nDevSquad is not only scalable and fully managed, but also has systems in place to help you take the reins of product management and QA, when you’re ready to do so. \n\n2. Boutique development firm with limited capacity\n\nA boutique dev firm is also fully managed, but not as scalable.\n\nWhat this type of software development agency offers\n\nBoutique development firms might also offer all of the above custom software development services in order to see a software project from start to finish.\n\nHowever, what is offered can vary widely depending on the agency’s internal skills. Achieving high-quality digital products can be hit or miss—or just costly.\n\nPros and cons\n\nWhen working with a boutique agency, you can often get a great client experience, and rest assured that the project is being taken care of end to end. But that greatly depends on what the team can provide. \n\nThese companies tend to have just a few employees and to not work with many contractors. There are often very long waitlists, as the CEOs of boutique agencies are looking for a nice business, but not interested in scaling. \n\nBecause they manage everything for you, they may or may not have systems in place to help you take ownership of the software product when the time comes. \n\nExample: Ikayzo\n\nBased out of Honolulu, Ikayzo is a boutique agency that offers web app and mobile app development along with website design and development. The company has 11 full time employees, so it’s not as widely scalable. \n\nBoutiques like these can yield very skilled resources, but they might have long waitlists and they might lack the type of talent you need. \n\n3. Staffing agency with no management\n\nNow, let’s look at agencies that help you staff developers. \n\nWhat this type of software development agency offers\n\nThis type of agency offers either:\n\nOngoing service fees for working with developers\n\nOne-time finding fee for hiring developers\n\nIn any case, they do not offer end-to-end software development. They don’t include UX design, QA management, or DevOps along with the software engineering resources.\n\nMost importantly, they don’t manage these resources for you. They just give you access to devs, and it’s up to you to produce quality software. \n\nPros and cons\n\nEmployment of software developers is expected to grow by 22% from 2019 to 2029, which is far faster than most other types of jobs. A staffing agency can help you find and hire the right software developers, but that’s only half the battle.\n\nBecause a staffing agency doesn’t help you assemble and manage high-performing product teams, that burden falls on you and your internal resources. \n\nTo craft beautiful software that works, you need not only software engineers, but also product managers, DevOps engineers, UX designers, and QA testers. \n\nExample: Toptal\n\nToptal is a well-known software development agency, but as one of our clients pointed out to us, it’s more of a staffing agency than anything else. \n\nToptal does not offer end-to-end software development services, leaving it up to their clients to handle business strategy, product management, QA management, and more.\n\nThis model could be an okay fit for you if you have world-class product managers on staff, but if you don’t, you run the risk of losing your entire investment in software engineering because you lack the other skills required to support, launch, and maintain their work. \n\n5 common business models employed by software development agencies\n\nUnderstanding the business model of a software development agency is just as important as understanding its type. While the agency type tells you what kind of service model to expect (fully managed, boutique, or staffing), the business model tells you how that service is structured and delivered.\n\nHere's a breakdown of the five most common business models used across the industry:\n\n1. Dedicated team (fully managed)\n\nThe agency provides a complete team—product strategist, engineers, QA, DevOps, UX/UI—and handles every stage of the software lifecycle. This approach is hands-off for clients and focused on building and launching full products, not just code.\n\nBest for: Companies without in-house product or engineering leadership\n\nClient involvement: Low to moderate\n\nCommon with: Scalable, fully managed agencies like DevSquad and Intellias\n\n2. Staff augmentation\n\nThe agency supplies one or more developers who plug into your internal team. You remain responsible for managing the project, setting priorities, and coordinating other roles like design, QA, and DevOps.\n\nBest for: Teams with strong internal product management and technical leadership\n\nClient involvement: High\n\nCommon with: Staffing agencies like Toptal, YouTeam, and Flexiple\n\n3. Fixed-price projects\n\nUnder a fixed-price model, the agency and client agree upfront on a detailed scope of work, timeline, and cost. It works best when requirements won’t change—though in reality, product needs often evolve mid-project. Changes typically trigger added fees or delays.\n\nBest for: Simple, well-scoped projects or MVPs\n\nClient involvement: Low during execution, but risky if the scope needs to shift\n\nCommon with: Some boutique development firms\n\n4. Time & materials (T&M)\n\nHere, you’re billed based on actual time and effort. This model is often used in agile environments, where priorities shift and roadmaps are refined over time. It allows room to adapt but requires close collaboration and mutual trust between client and agency.\n\nBest for: Long-term, iterative development\n\nClient involvement: Moderate to high\n\nCommon with: Both boutique and scalable agencies\n\n5. Product strategy consulting (upfront discovery model)\n\nBefore any development starts, the agency leads a strategic discovery phase—validating your product idea, prototyping user flows, and building a roadmap. This model sets you up for a smarter build and reduces risk of building the wrong product.\n\nBest for: Founders validating new ideas or teams modernizing legacy software\n\nClient involvement: High at the start, then lower after strategy is defined\n\nCommon with: Strategy-first firms like DevSquad and ImaginaryCloud\n\nHow to choose the right type of software development agency for your needs\n\nNow that you’ve seen some of the top software development agencies, you might be wondering which one is right for you.\n\nRather than go straight into vetting different companies, your first step should be to decide which type of agency is the best fit for your needs. \n\nIn order to get clear on which category is right for you, answer the following questions:\n\nDo you need guidance in feature prioritization and product strategy?\n\nIf you know you need strategic guidance in business processes and making the right product decisions, then you’ll want to work with either a scalable, fully managed agency, or with a boutique agency. A staffing company will not be able to provide this.\n\nDo you need other resources besides software engineers?\n\nIf you need DevOps engineers, UX designers, and QA managers in addition to software developers, your money will be better spent at a fully managed agency or boutique agency. A staffing company will provide you with full time hires, and you may not have full time work for all of these resources.\n\nDo you have the capability and capacity to manage all required resources?\n\nIf you have an inhouse product manager who can pull all of these resources together to create a product your end-user will love, then you can probably do fine with a staffing agency. If not, work with a fully managed agency that provides product management. \n\nIf you can’t own product management now, do you hope to inhouse this in the future?\n\nMaybe you’re not able to take the reins of product management now, but you’d like to in the future. In that case, make sure you’re working with a fully managed agency that makes the transition smooth for you. \n\nUltimately, before you choose the right agency, you first need to get clear on the type of agency you’re looking for. This will help ensure that your investment is a success. \n\nTop 50 software development companies to consider\n\nNow that you know the main types of software development agencies (and how to choose the right one), you're armed with lots of knowledge to help you vet companies.
