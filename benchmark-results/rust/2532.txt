{"title":"CS 224 Fall 23","author":null,"date":null,"main_content":"Algorithms for Data Science\n\nCS 224, Fall 2023\n\nCourse Details\n\nThis is a graduate-level topics class on algorithmic challenges arising in modern machine learning and data science more broadly. The course will touch upon a number of well-studied problems (generative modeling, deep learning theory, adversarial robustness, inverse problems, inference) and frameworks for algorithm design (gradient descent, spectral methods, tensor/moment methods, message passing, convex programming hierarchies, Markov chains). As we will see, proving rigorous guarantees for these problems often draws upon a wide range of techniques from stochastic calculus, harmonic analysis, random matrix theory, algebra, and statistical physics. We will also explore the myriad modeling challenges that go into building theory for ML and discuss prominent paradigms (semi-random models, smoothed analysis, oracles) for going beyond traditional worst-case analysis.\n\nTime/Location: MW 2:15-3:30, SEC LL2-224\n\nInstructor: Sitan Chen (sitan@seas.harvard.edu)\n\nOffice hours: SEC 3.325, Thursday 4-5\n\nTeaching Fellows:\n\nDavid Brewster (dbrewster@g.harvard.edu), Office hours: Maxwell-Dworkin 123, Tuesday 5-6\n\nDepen Morwani (dmorwani@g.harvard.edu), Office hours: SEC 1.404, Wednesday 11-12\n\nRosie Zhao (rosiezhao@g.harvard.edu), Office hours: SEC 2.330, Monday 11-12\n\nPset office hour: SEC 3.314, Thursday 4-6 on weeks with a pset deadline\n\nCanvas (for lecture recordings and announcements)\n\nGradescope\n\nEd (discussion)\n\nCourse Policies: See syllabus for detailed overview.\n\nStudent projects\n\nYuyang Zhang: Difficulties and Solutions in Learning Latent Variable Models\n\nPrashanth Amireddy: Unsupervised Learning via Algebraic Circuit Reconstruction\n\nItai Shapira: Edge of Stability Training Dynamics\n\nAlan Chung, Ben Schiffer, Kevin Luo: Non-Asymptotic Convergence of Diffusion Models\n\nAlexander Cai, Oliver Cheng, Tianze Jiang: Mean-field approximation and correlation rounding\n\nShi Feng, Xiaodong Yang: Posterior Sampling via Diffusion Processes\n\nArif Kerem Dayi: Leap Complexity: How SGD Exploits Hierarchical Structure in Functions to Learn Efficiently\n\nAnnouncements\n\n10/24: Pset 4 (final) is out, see below.\n\n10/10: Pset 3 is out, see below.\n\n10/2: See canvas announcement for information about final project.\n\n9/25: Pset 2 is out, see below.\n\n9/11: Pset 1 is out, see below.\n\nSee Canvas for link to sign up for pset office hour preferences\n\nNotifications of course placement will be sent out Thursday afternoon, Aug 31.\n\nAssignments\n\nMiscellaneous Materials\n\nHelpful references:\n\nRoman Vershynin.
