{"title":"Food Prices for Nutrition DataHub: global statistics on the Cost and Affordability of Healthy Diets","author":null,"date":null,"main_content":"The  Food Prices for Nutrition DataHub  provides access to global statistics on the cost and affordability of healthy diets and related indicators. These data use food item availability and prices from the  International Comparison Program (ICP), combined with food composition data and nutritional requirements from a wide range of sources including the Healthy Diet Basket based on national dietary guidelines.\n\nThe 42 indicators and their metadata cover:\n\nDiet cost\nCost of a healthy diet, per person per day\nCost of an energy sufficient diet and a nutrient adequate diet, per person per day\nCost per person per day, cost share, and cost relative to starchy staples of different food groups \n\nDiet affordability\nPrevalence of unaffordability and number of people unable to afford each diet\nRatio of each diet cost to observed national average food expenditures\nRatio of each diet cost to the food component of the international poverty lines as defined by the World Bank's income group classification\n\nFor further information on definitions and methods please refer to  Methodology. \n\n[  Back to page top  ] \n\nDATA HIGHLIGHTS\nGlobal improvement, uneven progress: Access to healthy diets is improving worldwide, but large regional disparities remain.\n2024 global cost: The average cost of a healthy diet reached $4.46 per person per day (PPP) in 2024.\nGlobal affordability: Around 2.6 billion people could not afford a healthy diet in 2024, 48.8 million fewer than in 2023.\nRegional challenges:\nSub-Saharan Africa and low-income countries faced two consecutive years of worsening affordability since 2022.\nFood inflation in these regions has eased only marginally, leaving diet costs near record highs.\nA somewhat worsening trend has also been observed in the Middle East and North Africa, Afghanistan and Pakistan region.\nThe cost of each of the six food groups making up the cost of a healthy diet in 2021 is also provided.\nThe most expensive food group is animal-sourced foods, priced on average at $1.00 for the daily quantity needed in a healthy diet. The highest cost is in low-income countries at $1.31 and in Sub-Saharan Africa at $1.22.\nVegetables are the second most expensive at $0.76 globally, with Latin America & Caribbean having the highest cost of $1.02.\nFruits cost $0.67 on average, and East Asia and Pacific has the highest cost of $0.95. \n\n[  Back to page top  ]"}
