It is the process of making your website more visible in search engines like Google without paying for ads.\n\nSEO is sometimes called SEO marketing, SEO optimization, or organic search marketing. It can help you earn organic (unpaid) rankings like this:\n\nAs a result, you can gain more brand awareness and traffic.\n\nSEO involves making your site more appealing to both users and search engines.\n\nThe four main elements in a well-rounded SEO strategy are:\nKeyword Research: Finding out what your target audience searches for\nOn-Page SEO: Optimizing webpages and their content\nOff-Page SEO: Getting other sites to link to your site, which can boost its reputation\nTechnical SEO: Improving your site's overall performance and accessibility\n\nYou will learn more about these elements later.\n\nSearch Engine Marketing vs. Search Engine Optimization\n\nSearch engine marketing (SEM) promotes a business through unpaid or paid results on search engines, often both.\nSEO is the subset of SEM that focuses on organic results. It generates traffic that you don’t have to pay the search engine for.\nPay-per-click ( PPC ) marketing is the subset of SEM that focuses on sponsored (paid) results. It involves setting up ad campaigns, paying to run them, and generating traffic.\n\nPaid results don’t always appear on search engine results pages ( SERPs ). When they do, they usually appear at the top with a \"Sponsored\" or \"Ad\" label.\n\nLike this:\n\nPPC can give you immediate visibility for high-value keywords, whereas SEO can take weeks or months to show results.\n\nHowever, organic results tend to have higher click-through rates because users often trust them more than ads. Organic results can also generate sustained traffic at little to no ongoing cost.\n\nWhy Is SEO Important?\n\nSEO is important in digital marketing because it helps you capitalize on the organic brand awareness and traffic that search engines generate.\n\nFor example, this brand’s SEO strategy yields around 7.4 million monthly visitors, according to Semrush’s Organic Research tool:\n\nIf you don’t use SEO, you’re unlikely to rank prominently for relevant terms. This limits your ability to reach new prospects and persuade them to convert.\n\nUsers will probably go to a competitor instead.\n\nA higher ranking typically translates into more impressions and a better click-through rate.\n\nSince website SEO improves the user experience, it can also lead to better engagement and conversion rates, plus increased traffic from other sources—not just search engines.\n\nUltimately, SEO can deliver a good return on investment.\n\nHow Does SEO Work?\n\nSEO works by aligning your website with how search engines define good search results.\n\nSearch engines use complex processes and criteria to rank results, aiming to provide users with the best search experience.\n\nBy optimizing your website and content according to these systems, you can gain more visibility.\n\nHowever, SEO is not a perfect science.\n\nSearch engines are somewhat secretive about the systems they use and constantly change them to improve search results.\n\nTherefore, testing, analysis, and adaptation are crucial to SEO success.\n\nHow Do Search Engines Work?\n\nSearch engines work by crawling, indexing, and ranking content on the internet.\n\nHere’s what each process involves:\nCrawling: Search engines use automated programs called bots or crawlers to explore the web and discover content. They primarily follow hyperlinks to find new pages.\nIndexing: After analyzing the content, the search engine determines if it’s eligible for indexing. If it is, the content is added to the database of possible search results.\nRanking: When a user enters a query, the search engine tries to understand the search intent. It then uses a complex algorithm to present the best results from its index.\n\nWhen your content appears in search results, it can take various forms, such as traditional search listings (the standard blue links).\n\nGoogle also displays SERP features (non-standard results) to enhance the search experience.\n\nFor example, this SERP includes a popular products section:\n\nThe SERP also includes rich snippets, which are normal results with extra elements like images and star ratings:\n\nFactors that influence your presence, positioning, and appearance on the SERP are known as ranking factors. They include:\nThe relevancy and quality of your content\nThe presence of keywords on your page\nThe number and quality of links from other sites, which can boost your reputation\nHow you use schema markup to help search engines understand and categorize information on your site\nThe speed and usability of your site\n\nTypes of SEO\n\nHere are some of the most important types of search engine optimization marketing:\n\nOn-Page SEO\n\nOn-page SEO is the process of optimizing webpages to improve their search visibility. It focuses largely on using keywords effectively.\n\nKeywords are relevant search terms you want the page to rank for. They help show search engines and users that your content is relevant to their search.\n\nIncorporating your keywords into the body content and the following locations when it is natural to do so is best practice:\nTitle tag: The HTML page title that can appear in SERPs\nMeta description: The HTML page summary that can appear in SERPs\nH1 tag: The HTML element that contains the on-page title\nSubheading tags: HTML tags that contain subheadings (H2s, H3s, etc.)\nAlt text: HTML image descriptions\nURL slug: The unique part of the webpage address\n\nAvoiding keyword stuffing—forcing in keywords where they do not belong—is also important because Google considers this a spammy practice. Violating Google's spam policies can lead to a penalty that harms your search visibility.\n\nOff-Page SEO\n\nOff-page SEO refers to actions you can take outside of your website to improve its search visibility.\n\nIt most commonly involves link building, which is the process of getting other high-quality websites to link to your website.\n\nThese links are called backlinks, and they can help your SEO by signaling that your website is useful and trustworthy.\n\nOther off-page tactics like social media management, online reputation management, and email marketing can indirectly benefit your SEO by driving brand awareness and engagement.\n\nTechnical SEO\n\nTechnical SEO involves improving the technical aspects of your website to enhance its search visibility.\n\nEnsuring that search engines can crawl and index your website properly is the most important aspect of technical SEO. This may involve:\nUsing meta robots tags and robots.txt to control the behavior of search engine bots\nCreating an XML sitemap to help search engines understand your site structure\nBuilding internal links to assist search bots and users in navigating your site\n\nAfter ensuring proper crawling and indexing, focus on user experience factors such as page speed, website security, and mobile-friendliness.\n\nImage SEO\n\nImage SEO is the process of optimizing images on your website to make them appear more prominently in search results and enhance your overall SEO performance.\n\nFor example, image optimization can help you gain exposure like this:\n\nCommon image SEO tactics include:\nResizing and compressing images to improve page load speed\nAdding alt text (HTML image descriptions) to help search engines and users with screen readers understand what images depict\nEnsuring images are visually appealing and useful to your audience\n\nAccording to Semrush Sensor data from September 2024, Google displays images in the top 20 results for over half of U.S. SERPs. Many users also go directly to Google Images.\n\nTherefore, image SEO provides a significant opportunity to drive traffic to your website.\n\nVideo SEO\n\nVideo SEO is the process of creating and optimizing videos for maximum visibility in search results. This includes results on search engines like Google and video platforms like YouTube.\n\nCommon video SEO tactics include:\nPerforming YouTube keyword research to discover what videos users search for\nAdding keywords to your video title and description\nUsing schema markup to help search engines understand embedded videos\n\nLocal SEO\n\nLocal SEO is the process of improving your visibility in location-based search results.\n\nFor example, when someone searches \"outdoor store near me\" or \"[CITY] outdoor store\":\n\nLocal SEO is important if you serve customers in a specific area or have brick-and-mortar locations people can visit.\n\nOne of the most important factors in local SEO is optimizing your Google Business Profile because Google evaluates and ranks profiles (rather than webpages) in map-based results.\n\nOther tactics include:\nListing your business in online directories\nEncouraging and responding to Google reviews\nPublishing localized content on your website\n\nHow to Get Started with SEO\n\nTo understand SEO and how it works, you need to put it into practice.
