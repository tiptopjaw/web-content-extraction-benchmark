For an eye-pleasing, symmetrical setup, place key seating pieces facing each other near the fireplace. Place end tables at both ends of the sofa for added convenience. Balance the visual weight of the sofa with paired chairs to maximize seating. This layout accommodates guests while maintaining a chill, cozy vibe.\n\n04\nof 26\n\nFloating Furniture Arrangement\n\nWith no free walls and a central fireplace, arrange seating in the center of the room. Position chairs and sofas facing each other to invite conversation between guests. Use a plush rug and large coffee table to anchor the setup. Allow 30 inches between furniture that you need to be able to walk around, and maintain 14 to 18 inches between a coffee table and sofa so drinks are within reaching distance. This rule still applies when planning a large living room furniture arrangement, because if a piece of furniture is too far away, it might look like it's floating in the room without purpose.\n\n08\nof 26\n\nFurniture Arranging Ideas for Accent Tables\n\nThe key to a good furniture arrangement is in details like side tables and coffee tables. The remaining ⅓ of the room should be a balance of free floor space and smaller storage or accent pieces.\n\nWhat are some common mistakes in arranging furniture?\n\nPushing all of the furniture up against the wall is a common decorating mistake. Choosing lots of small pieces for a small room is another common mistake. Having fewer large pieces will create a more streamlined look.\n\nAre there any tools that can help me arrange my furniture?\n\nYes there are low tech and high tech tools that you can use to help figure out your floor plan.
