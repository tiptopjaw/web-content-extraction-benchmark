She also specializes in family travel, maximizing your paid time off, working around school breaks and schedules, and finding great deals during high-peak travel seasons.\n\nThis article has been viewed 332,711 times.\n\nIf you’re traveling soon, booking a flight is one of the most important parts of finalizing your plans. But between airlines constantly changing prices and the many options of where to purchase your flight, booking can get a little confusing. The following methods will help you successfully book the best flight for your upcoming trip.\n\nBest Tips for Booking a Flight\n\nFamily travel expert Shelby Reyes recommends using Google flights to check out typical ticket prices for when you want to travel and setting up alerts to find out when prices change. Outline your travel plans beforehand to get tickets that fit your schedule. Then, compare the flight times, prices, and what they offer.\n\nSteps\n\nMethod 1\n\nMethod 1 of 2:\n\nBooking a Flight Online\n\n1\n\nOutline your tentative travel plans. Think about where you plan or might like to travel, the dates you would like to go, if you just want to book flights or a package deal.\n\nMake a list of your plans and have them handy and easily accessible while you book.\n\n2\n\nConsider being flexible in your plans. The more flexible you are on everything from departure and arrival airlines and airports to travel dates and package deals, the more likely you are to get a great deal on your flight. [1]\nX\nExpert Source\n\nAngela Rice\nTravel Specialist\n\nExpert Interview\n\nWednesdays are the generally the cheapest day to travel.. [2]\nX\nResearch source\n\nYou can often find good deals on last minute flights, especially if you buy in conjunction with a hotel and/or rental car (known as a package deal). [3]\nX\nResearch source\n\nFlying from alternative airports can often be cheaper and offer better connection times than large airport hubs. [4]\nX\nResearch source\n\nFor example, if you want to go to Washington, DC, consider flying to Baltimore Washington International Airport (BWI) instead of National Airport (DCA) or Dulles International (IAD). BWI is just slightly outside of Washington and has excellent transportation into the city from the airport.\n\nReader Poll: We asked 333 wikiHow readers how far in advance they like to get to the airport, and 47% of them said 2 hours. [Take Poll]\n\nConsider how early you want to be at the airport when choosing your flight time.\n\nAdvertisement\n\n3\n\nCompare flight prices. How much a flight costs varies greatly depending on many variables including the day you book, how far in advance you book, and even the website on which you book. [5]\nX\nResearch source\n\nBy comparing prices from different sites, you are likely to get the best flight deal.\n\nBook approximately six weeks in advance if you can. This will generally give you the best flight options and prices. [6]\nX\nResearch source\n\n[7]\nX\nResearch source\n\nTuesdays around 3pm Eastern time are the cheapest time to book your flight. [8]\nX\nResearch source\n\nTravel sites collate information on the best flight prices and available times. These include Kayak, Expedia, CheapTickets, and Priceline. Travel sites automatically allow you to compare prices and factor in travel variables.\n\nIt’s a good idea to compare travel site prices as well, as their respective offers can also vary greatly.\n\nAirline websites are also an excellent place to book tickets. It’s not uncommon to find cheaper and better flights on airline sites. [9]\nX\nResearch source\n\nFor more options, consider one way travel on different airlines for each leg of your trip. [10]\nX\nResearch source\n\n4\n\nKeep a list of flight fares and offers. As you compare flight offers, keep a list of all relevant details including departure and arrival airports and times, fees, and cancellation policies. This will help you more easily decide on the right flight to purchase.\n\nNote if the price includes such items as taxes and baggage fees.\n\nRead each flight’s cancellation policies and change fees. Not knowing these in advance can cost you a lot of money and time if you need to cancel or change your flight.\n\n5\n\nPurchase your ticket. Once you decide on the right flight for your upcoming travel, it’s time to buy your ticket.\n\nFollow websites prompts. Every site will ask you to fill in information on items such as your name, number of travels, frequent flyer number, seat and meal preferences, and credit card information in order to book.\n\nYou can usually pay baggage fees and select seats during your booking sessions. It’s a good idea to do this in advance to minimize your time checking in at the airport.\n\nIf you’re traveling internationally, you will need a passport to confirm the reservation.\n\nDecide if you want to pay for extras such as upgrades in seat class or travel insurance.\n\nMany travel and airline sites will offer further special deals for add-ons such as rental car or hotel room.\n\n6\n\nPrint booking confirmation and other relevant documents. Make sure to take these documents to the airport with you on the day of your flight to avoid any questions or problems with your booking.\n\nFollow the “24-hour rule.” Within 24 hours of booking your flight, check the prices one last time. If the fare has decreased, call the airline and rebook the flight at the lower price with no penalty. [11]\nX\nResearch source\n\nAdvertisement\n\nMethod 2\n\nMethod 2 of 2:\n\nBooking with an Airline or Travel Agent\n\n1\n\nOutline your tentative travel plans. Just as with online booking, think about where you plan or would like to travel, the dates you would like to go, if you just want to book flights or maybe a package deal.\n\nMake a list of your plans and have them available while you talk to the travel or airline agent.\n\n2\n\nContact a travel agent or an airline representative. You can call either traditional travel agents or airline reps to help you find the best flight booking.\n\nGive the agent the information on your tentative travel plans. Also let them know pertinent information including your seat preferences, and if you are flexible in your travel dates and airlines.\n\nLike with online booking, consider being flexible in your plans for the best prices and times. [12]\nX\nResearch source\n\nA good agent will alert you to all of the variables in booking your flight like alternative airports and smaller airlines. They’ll allow you to decide what offer is best for yourself.\n\n3\n\nCompare prices from different agents. Call several travel agents and ask them for price quotes. By comparing what different agents offer, you will get the best flight deal.\n\nIf you find an agent you like but they don’t have the best offer, let them know about the lower price you found and see if they can match or better it.\n\n4\n\nPurchase your ticket. Once you decide on the right flight offer for your upcoming travel, it’s time to buy your ticket.\n\nCall the agent and let them know which flight you’d like to book. Answer any questions they may have about items such as available seats or your meal preferences.\n\nAsk questions about the booking. Find out about extra fees including taxes, baggage fees, and upgrade costs. Ask about cancellation and refund policies.\n\n5\n\nGet a copy of your booking confirmation and other relevant documents. Make sure to take these documents to the airport with you on the day of your flight to avoid any questions or problems with your booking.\n\nAdvertisement\n\nCommunity Q&A\n\nSearch\n\nAdd New Question\n\nQuestion\n\nHow long before a flight should I show up at the airport?\n\nCommunity Answer\n\nIt depends on the airport, but the general rule is at least two hours in advance.\n\nThanks!
