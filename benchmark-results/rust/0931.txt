{"title":"6 steps for first-time tax filers","author":null,"date":"2025-02-10T00:00:00+00:00","main_content":"Getting a sense of which credits and deductions you may be eligible for can help you pull together the proper documentation. Here are a few to consider:\nSaver’s credit. If you are not a full-time student and are not being claimed as a dependent, you may be eligible for a tax credit if you contribute to a retirement plan. The amount of the credit depends on your filing status and adjusted gross income. For the 2025 tax year, if your filing status is single, you may be eligible if your adjusted gross income is $39,500 or less. If you are married and are filing jointly, you may be eligible if your adjusted gross income is $79,000 or less. However, these numbers are subject to change in future tax years.\nStudent loan interest.  You may deduct up to $2,500 in interest payments, depending on your modified adjusted gross income.\n\nCharitable deductions. Donating to your alma mater or a favorite charity? Generally, you may deduct qualified charitable donations if you itemize your federal income tax return.\nFreelance expenses.  If you are self-employed, you may be able to claim deductions for work-related expenses such as industry subscriptions and office supplies.\n\nIf you think you may qualify for additional credits or deductions, check the  IRS website or consult with your personal tax advisor."}
