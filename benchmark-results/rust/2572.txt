{"title":"What is Printed Circuit Board (PCB)","author":"托普法斯特","date":"2025-06-04T06:26:43+00:00","main_content":"Table of Contents\n\nWhat is a Printed Circuit Board (PCB)?\n\nPCB (Printed Circuit Boards), also referred to as “printed wiring boards” or “printed wiring cards,” are the backbone of modern electronics, designed to interconnect and support electronic components while facilitating the transmission of signals and power.\n\nThe Need for PCB\n\nBefore PCBs, circuits relied on inefficient connection methods:\nPoint-to-Point Wiring: Prone to failures, with insulation degradation leading to short circuits.\n\nWire-Wrapping: Durable but labor-intensive, involving hand-wound wires around posts.\n\nAs electronics transitioned from vacuum tubes to silicon chips and integrated circuits (ICs), traditional methods became impractical, driving the adoption of printed circuit boards (PCB).\n\nPCB Structure and Function\nMaterials: Insulating substrate layered with conductive copper traces.\n\nKey Roles:\n\nElectrical Connectivity: Copper pathways facilitate signal and power transfer.\n\nMechanical Support: Secures components; solder (a metal alloy) bonds parts both electrically and physically.\n\nAdvantages of PCBs\nReliability: Eliminates manual wiring errors and aging-related failures.\n\nScalability: Enables mass production, reducing device size and cost.\n\nPCBs revolutionized electronics, becoming foundational to modern industry.\n\nComposition and Structure of Printed Circuit Boards (PCBs)\n\n1. Substrate\nMaterials:\n\nFR4 (Fiberglass + Epoxy): Most common, provides rigidity; standard thickness is 1.6mm (0.063 inches).\n\nFlexible Substrates (e.g., Polyimide/Kapton): Used for bendable PCBs, withstands high temperatures, ideal for specialized applications.\n\nLow-Cost Alternatives (Phenolic/Epoxy Resins): Found in budget consumer electronics; poor heat resistance, emits strong odors when soldered.\n\n2. Conductive Layer (Copper Foil)\nStructure:\n\nSingle-Sided: Copper on one side only (lowest cost).\n\nDouble-Sided: Copper on both sides (most common).\n\nMultilayer: Alternating conductive and insulating layers (up to 32+ layers).\n\nCopper Thickness Standards:\n\nStandard: 1 oz/ft² (~35 µm).\n\nHigh-power applications: 2–3 oz/ft² for increased current capacity.\n\n3. Solder Mask\nFunction:\n\nInsulates copper traces to prevent short circuits.\n\nGuides soldering (e.g., exposes pads through openings).\n\nColor: Typically green (e.g., SparkFun uses red), but customizable (blue, black, white, etc.).\n\n4. Silkscreen Layer\nPurpose: Labels component designators, polarity, test points, etc., aiding assembly and debugging.\n\nColor: Usually white, but other options exist (black, red, yellow, etc.).\n\nPCB Layer Structure Overview\nSingle-Sided: Substrate → Copper → Solder Mask → Silkscreen.\n\nDouble-Sided: Substrate (copper on both sides) → Solder Mask → Silkscreen.\n\nMultilayer: Alternating substrate/copper layers, topped with solder mask and silkscreen.\n\nPCB Substrate Materials Selection Guide\n\n1. Low-Cost Solutions (Consumer Electronics)\nFR-1/FR-2 (Phenolic Cotton Paper, aka “Bakelite”)\n\nMaterial: Phenolic resin + paper base\n\nFeatures: Ultra-low cost (~1/3 of FR-4), but poor heat resistance (prone to scorching) and mechanical strength\n\nApplications: Remote controls, toys, and other low-end electronics\n\n2. Standard Industrial-Grade Material\nFR-4 (Fiberglass Epoxy)\n\nMarket Share: Used in >80% of conventional PCBs\n\nAdvantages: Balanced cost/performance, heat resistance up to 130°C, standard 1.6mm thickness\n\nVariants:\nFR-3 (Paper-epoxy composite): Mid-range between FR-2 and FR-4\n\nFR-5: High-temperature enhanced version (withstands >150°C)\n\n3. High-Frequency Applications (>1GHz)\nPTFE (Teflon-Based Substrates)\n\nProperties: Extremely low dielectric loss (Dk=2.2), suitable for 5GHz+ mmWave\n\nExample Models: Rogers RO3000 series\n\nApplications: 5G base stations, satellite communications, radar systems\n\n4. High Thermal Conductivity Requirements\n\nMaterial Type Thermal Conductivity (W/mK) Typical Applications\nAluminum Clad 1-3 LED lighting, power modules\nCeramic (Al₂O₃) 20-30 Automotive LiDAR, aerospace\nCopper Clad 400 High-power IGBT modules\n\n5. Specialized Solutions\nCeramic Substrates (Alumina)\n\nAdvantages: Matches chip CTE, withstands 500°C\n\nProcessing: Requires laser drilling (high cost), e.g., Rogers RO4000\n\nComposite Materials (CEM Series)\n\nCEM-1: Paper core + fiberglass surface (FR-1 alternative)\n\nCEM-3: Fiberglass mat + epoxy resin (semi-transparent, common in Japan)\n\nTypes of Printed Circuit Board (PCB)\n\nPCBs are primarily classified into three fundamental types based on their layer structure:\nSingle-Layer PCB\nFeatures conductive copper on only one side of the substrate\n\nSimplest and most cost-effective design\n\nCommon applications: Basic electronics, calculators, power supplies\nDouble-Layer PCB\nConductive copper layers on both sides of the substrate\n\nThrough-hole vias connect circuits between layers\n\nOffers more complex routing than a single-layer\n\nTypical uses: Industrial controls, automotive dashboards\nMulti-Layer PCB\nStacked structure with alternating conductive and insulating layers (4-32+ layers)\n\nUses blind/buried vias for interlayer connections\n\nAdvantages: High density, improved EMI shielding\n\nApplications: Smartphones, servers, medical equipment\n\nFunctions of PCB Boards\n\n1. Electrical Connection\nFunctionality: Copper traces precisely connect components (resistors, capacitors, ICs, etc.) to form complete circuit topologies.\n\nTechnical Advantages:\n\nHigh Reliability: Replaces manual wiring, eliminating risks of short circuits/open circuits (e.g., smartphone motherboards with 0.1mm trace precision).\n\nSignal Integrity: Multilayer designs (e.g., 6+ layers) use ground/power planes to reduce crosstalk (critical for high-frequency communication devices).\n\nExample: Computer motherboards enable high-speed data transfer (e.g., PCIe 4.0 lanes) between CPU, RAM, and GPU via PCB routing.\n\n2. Mechanical Support\nStructural Design:\n\nRigid/Flex Options: Consumer electronics use FR4 rigid boards, while wearables adopt flexible PCBs (e.g., Apple Watch’s bendable circuits).\n\nMounting Methods: Mixed SMT (e.g., 0402 resistors) and THT (e.g., power connectors) layouts balance density and durability.\n\nPractical Value: Drone flight controllers achieve weight reduction and vibration resistance via lightweight PCB designs (e.g., aluminum substrates).\n\n3. Circuit Protection\nProtection Mechanisms:\n\nInsulating Substrate: FR4 materials withstand up to 500V/mm, preventing leakage (e.g., power adapter PCBs).\n\nSolder Mask: Green epoxy coating prevents oxidation/shorts (common around USB ports).\n\nSpecial Treatments: Automotive PCBs use conformal coating (anti-humidity, anti-corrosion) for harsh environments.\n\n4. Thermal Management\nCooling Techniques:\n\nCopper Heat Spreading: 2oz thick copper in LED driver boards reduces junction temperatures.\n\nThermal Optimization: Server motherboards employ thermal vias + pads to transfer heat to enclosures (e.g., Intel Xeon boards).\n\nSpecial Materials: Ceramic substrates (e.g., aluminum nitride, 170W/mK) for high-power IGBT modules.\n\n5. Space Optimization\nAdvanced Processes:\n\nHDI Technology: Blind/buried vias enable 10-layer stacking in smartphone boards (e.g., iPhone’s Any-layer HDI).\n\nVia-in-Pad: JLCPCB’s resin-filled vias prevent solder leakage under BGA chips (e.g., Snapdragon processors).\n\nCost Efficiency: Compact layouts (e.g., smartwatch PCBs at 20mm×30mm) reduce unit costs.\n\nExtended Applications\nHigh-Frequency: 5G base station PCBs use PTFE (ε=2.2) to minimize signal loss.\n\nHigh-Reliability: Aerospace PCBs with 50μm gold plating ensure long-term stability.\n\nThrough material, process, and design innovations, PCBs continue to drive electronics toward higher performance, miniaturization, and reliability.\n\nPCB Manufacturing Process Detailed Explanation\n\nSingle-Layer PCB PCB Design\nSoftware Tools: CAD tools (e.g., Altium Designer, KiCad, Eagle) define circuit layout, traces, and component placement.\n\nDesign Output: Gerber files (for fabrication) and BOM (Bill of Materials) are generated.\n\nOEM Role: Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs) finalize the design before sending it to PCB manufacturers.\n\n2. PCB Fabrication\n\nThe design is transformed into a physical board through:\nEtching: Copper layers are chemically etched to form conductive traces.\n\nDrilling: Holes are drilled for vias and through-hole components (mechanical or laser drilling).\n\nLamination: Multilayer PCBs are bonded under heat and pressure.\n\nSurface Finish: Options include HASL (Hot Air Solder Leveling), ENIG (Electroless Nickel Immersion Gold), and OSP (Organic Solderability Preservative).\n\n3. PCB Assembly (PCBA)\n\nComponents are mounted onto the PCB using:\n\nA. Through-Hole Technology (THT)\nComponents have leads inserted into drilled holes.\n\nSoldered on the opposite side (wave soldering or manual soldering).\n\nPros: Strong mechanical bonds, high reliability.\n\nCons: Larger footprint, slower assembly.\n\nB. Surface-Mount Technology (SMT)\nComponents are placed directly onto PCB pads.\n\nProcess:\nSolder Paste Application: Stencil printing deposits paste on pads.\n\nPick-and-Place: Robots position components with high precision.\n\nReflow Soldering: The board is heated to melt solder paste.\nPros: Smaller size, faster assembly, better for high-frequency circuits.\n\nCons: Requires precise machinery, harder to rework.\n\nC. Mixed Assembly (SMT + THT)\nSome boards combine both methods (e.g., large connectors in THT, ICs in SMT).\n\n4. Testing & Quality Control\nAutomated Optical Inspection (AOI): Checks for soldering defects.\n\nIn-Circuit Testing (ICT): Validates electrical performance.\n\nFunctional Testing: Ensures the PCB works as intended.\n\nWhy Modern PCBs Prefer SMT?\nSmaller size (enables compact devices like smartphones).\n\nHigher component density (more functionality per unit area).\n\nFaster assembly (suitable for mass production).\n\nBetter high-frequency performance (shorter traces reduce EMI).\n\nPCB Components & Modern Design Trends\n\n1.
