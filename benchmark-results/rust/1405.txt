[9]\n\nLONGER ANSWER – watch this video:\n\nAs we cover in our “ How Many Sets and Reps? ” guide, a “set” is a series of repetitions that you complete without stopping.\n\nFor example, if you drop down and do 10 push-ups right now, you just did 1 SET of 10 REPETITIONS (or REPS) of push-ups.\n\nGot it? Cool.\n\nSome general rules on repetitions you can follow as you’re starting to build your workout plan:\n\nIf you’re looking to burn fat while building muscle, keep your number of repetitions per set in the 8-15 range per set.\n\nIf you can do more than 15 reps without much of a challenge, consider increasing the weight or the difficulty of the movement. This is true for things like lunges, bodyweight squats, push-ups, pull-ups, etc.\n\nIf you want to focus more on building strength, keep your repetitions in the 5-10 range per set. As you get comfortable with the movements, you can move into lower rep ranges – but we find for beginners that starting with slightly higher reps gives you more time to practice doing the movement correctly.\n\nThere are some other generally accepted ‘rules’ about how to determine how many reps you should target per set, based on your goals:\n\nReps in the 1-5 range build super dense muscle and strength (called myofibrillar hypertrophy).\n\nReps in the 6-12 range build a somewhat equal amount of muscular strength and muscular size (this is called sarcoplasmic hypertrophy). [35]\n\nReps in the 12+ range build muscular endurance.\n\nHowever, don’t think of these as hard and fast rules. For example, a 2015 study [10] called into question the best rep strategy for building muscle or size:\n\nIt appears that high-intensity resistance (sets of 3-5 reps) training stimulates greater improvements in some measures of strength and hypertrophy in resistance-trained men during a short-term training period [compared to sets of 8-10 reps].\n\nOther recent studies have found that people built a similar amount of muscle with anywhere from 5 to 30 repetitions, as long as they worked close to failure. [38]\n\nWhat this means: Do not freak yourself out by worrying if you should do 3 sets or 4 sets of 8 reps or 10 reps. \n\nOur advice would be to START with lighter weights and more reps as you learn the movement, and then decide if you want to stay at higher reps and lower weight or vice versa.\n\nYou do you, because either way will get you results!\n\nThe only thing you need to worry about: get stronger the next time you do that movement.\n\nEither pick up a heavier weight, or do 1 more repetition than last time.\n\n“JUST GIVE ME THE ANSWER!”\n\nKeep your TOTAL (all exercises combined) workout number of sets for all exercises in the 10-20 set range, with 5-15 reps per set:\n\n4 exercises total, each with 2 to 5 “work sets” is a good start. The key is being recovered enough that you can perform the next set at similar intensity and with great technique. Whatever it takes to get you there, do it!
