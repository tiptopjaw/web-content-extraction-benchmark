When used in combination they strike at the root causes of\nsoftware development problems. They are called \"best practices\" not because\nwe can precisely quantify their value but rather they are observed to be\ncommonly used in industry by successful organizations.\n\n  Best Practices address the root causes of poor software development \n\n  1. Develop Iteratively  \n \n  Critical risks are resolved before making large investments\n  Initial iterations enable early user feedback\n  Testing and integration are continuous\n  Objective milestones provide short term focus\n \n\n  2. Manage Requirements  \n \n  Requirements are dynamic - expect them to change during software\ndevelopment\n  User's own understanding of the requirements evolves over time\n Gain agreement with user on what the system should do and not how\n  Maintain forward and backward traceability of requirements\n \n\n  3. Use Component Based Architecture  \n \n  Using components permits reuse\n  Choice of thousands of commercially available components\n  Improved maintainability and extensibility\n  Promotes clean division of work among teams of developers\n \n\n  4. Visually Model Software  \n \n  Visual modeling improves our ability to manage software complexity\n  Capture the structure and behavior of components\n  Hide or expose details as appropriate for the task\n  Promote unambiguous communication\n \n\n  5. We model because we cannot\ncomprehend the complexity of a system in its entirety.\n  We model to visualize, specify, construct, and document the \nstructure and behavior of a system's architecture.\n  A model is a complete description of a system from a particular\nperspective.\n \n\n  Principles of Modeling  \n \n  The choice of what models to create has a profound influence on\nhow a problem is attacked and how a solution is shaped.\n  Every model maybe expressed at different levels of precision.\n  The best models are connected to reality.\n  No single model is sufficient.
