{"title":"Finding my balance: An evolved and simplified task management system","author":null,"date":null,"main_content":"Jul 30, 2023 | Reading Time: 5 min\n\nEdit: A next stage evolution of this system can be found in my follow up post\n\nhere\n\nEvolution\n\nIn this ever-changing world, with shifting demands and responsibilities, I have experimented through multiple task\nmanagement strategies. E.g: Zettelkasten (much more than task management, but still), Eisenhower matrix, Getting things\ndone (GTD) method, Agile/Kanban, ABCDE method, Franklin planner method, etc.\n\nFinally, I’ve settled on a system that works for me (in most cases). It’s most likely a mash-up of various methods\nrecommended from books, blog posts, conversations with experienced folks, etc. It may not be perfect and might even\nchange in the future with changing roles and responsibilities, but it has served me well since quite some time.\n\nGranularity\n\nI mostly deal with unit items of tasks which are neither too small, nor too large.\n\n“Steps” to complete one item are not included ( Not Too Small ).\n\nProjects or ideas that might span a long time (months to years) are excluded ( Not Too Large ). These items and\nassociated brainstorming don’t come into the task list. Parts of it only come here once these things materialize and\nare broken down into smaller units. Deliberation on a project, visions, designs, breakdowns into smaller items, are\noutside of task list.\n\nArrangement (prioritization)\n\nP-0: A very short list of things I would do “immediately”.\n\nItems that someone has told you to do immediately, but which you practically won’t be able to accomplish, should\nnot be included (i.e. give me yesterday things).\n\nStress Indicator: A large P-0 list means very high level of stress and is a sign of a problem that you need\nto address.\n\nP-1: Slightly larger list that contains the items that I need to address “sooner than later”.\n\nMany tasks enter directly into P-1.\n\nDuring peaceful times, P-0 is mostly populated from P-1.\n\nAlthough direct entries into P-0 can still happen, I prefer to avoid this as much as possible\n\nP-2: A large list of various obligations and tasks.\n\nThese may be things I think I have to do, but they have no associated urgency and questionable motivation.\n\nThis is an often neglected list. It turns out to be a “write and forget” list, with a hope attached that it\n“may” be used someday.\n\nGeneral preference is to move things from here to associated project/ideas pages and reevaluate at that level\nrather than in task list.\n\nP-X: A short list of things that excite me.\n\nKeeping it short is crucial.\n\nIf this grows, that means you I am not really excited by all of them and most probably few items need to be in\nP2.\n\nDaily focus (the heart of my system)\n\nEvery day come up with tasks in two categories (~5/10 minutes daily time spend):\n\nDo:\n\nThese are the tasks I have planned for the day, primarily a subset from P-0.\n\nMeetings are excluded from this for now. They tend to be obligatory and preplanned most of the time. I don’t see\na lot of value in adding them as tasks.\n\nLook forward to:\n\nItems that excite me. Generally items come from P-0 or P-X here.\n\nIf there are days when this list is empty, it’s a sign of a problem that I need to address.\n\nGood days are when there are more than 1 item here.\n\nTools\n\nTried and discarded:\n\nGoogle keep:\n\nIt served well enough in very early stages.\n\nSomehow the “features” added increased and its usefulness decreased.\n\nI still use it for lists or things I need to refer to once in a while, but not for tasks.\n\nRoam research/Notion:\n\nTried them for a very short time and most probably very early in their lifecycle.\n\nNever got over the initial barrier.\n\nEditor plugins\n\nWas interested in this as my writing in Markdown increased over time.\n\nMulti device availability and less suitability for projects/ideas deliberation were a problem.\n\nKanban boards\n\nTried it out just for the heck of it at some point.\n\nLooking at a board at the start of the day was a very bad feeling generally.\n\nCurrent:\n\nMicrosoft OneNote\n\nGot introduced to it via a colleague when I shifted to Mac from Linux.\n\nI can easily keep most things in one “Page”.\n\nDaily “Do/Look forward to” list can be created in minimal time.\n\nMoving around tasks is very easy.\n\nProject/ideas/Meeting notes pages can be kept in same place.\n\nSync is available across my devices (Mac/Android).\n\nThere are a lot of features there that I have almost never used. Hope is that the primary workflow and UX\nfor note-taking remains more or less same.\n\nParting note\n\nInterestingly, this model doesn’t apply to my family life at all. But, I find myself following it about 80% of the time\nin my work/career life. It’s a delicate balance between the immediate necessities and the sparks of excitement that keep\nme going.\n\nThe red flags, the problems, the overwhelming moments — all find their reflection in this system. It is not necessary\nthat this model reduces stress per se. Depending on the way my career was going the P-0 list was very overwhelming at\nsome points. And even though I was aware of the red flags, I couldn’t do much about it.\n\nI hope sharing this provides a window into my thoughts and methods, but it’s essential to remember that this is purely\nmy experience. What works for me might not be applicable to anyone else, as it’s a product of my unique journey and\nprofessional life."}
