How will you create this for yourself?\n\nHow will you ensure each day has a predictable rhythm?\n\nWake up and go to bed at about the same time daily\n\nTry alarms to keep you on schedule\n\nCheck out this video on time management and SMART goals!\n\nYou might also want to fill out this time inventory to help get started!\n\nHow will you use a calendar to keep you on track?\n\nMark when you need to be present for real-time online classes, for instructor-led review sessions, or for study groups with peers\n\nUpdate your calendar if test dates and other due dates have been changed. You can use this assignment and test tracker, too!\n\nThis might also be a good time to look at the big picture with our Goal Tracker!\n\nHow will you create a study schedule?\n\nMark your calendar with “appointments” for independent studying, reading, and assignment completion for each class\n\nCheck out our Weekly Action Plan! It’s a chart you can use to get started\n\nStudy space\n\nEarmarking a space dedicated for study will help you stay organized and productive. What will your study space look like?\n\nWhat enhances your overall focus and productivity?\n\nExplore if you work best with quiet or ambient noise\n\nTry turning off your phone for set periods of time\n\nInternet blocking sites can help you stay away from time-wasters online\n\nCheck out these resources on distractions overall and digital distractions in particular\n\nHow will you minimize distractions from family members or roommates?\n\nTry a white noise machine or app to drown out voices\n\nUse noise cancelling headphones\n\nCreate agreements with others about when and where you’ll study\n\nHow will you stay engaged and alert?\n\nIf you’re studying in a bedroom and you’re inclined to nap, avoid studying on your bed\n\nUse the Pomodoro Technique to study in short segments and then take a short break. Repeat this process.\n\nWatch this video on evidence-based study strategies to learn more!\n\nHow will you keep your materials organized?\n\nTry separate folders for each class—both digital and hard-copy\n\nTake a few minutes at the end of each day to sort through papers, files, emails, etc. Anything need to be tossed, deleted, refiled?\n\nSetting goals\n\nStarting each day and each week with clear and concrete goals will give your days purpose and structure.\n\nHere are two handouts for setting priorities to get you started. One is focused on what you decide that you must do, should do, could do, and the other designates priority alphabetically with an A, B, C, and D scheduling model.\n\nAny large papers or projects due at the end of the semester?\n\nBreak down projects into a series of discrete steps, and schedule these steps into your calendar. Try working backwards from the due date.\n\nWhat do you want and need to accomplish for each class in the coming week?\n\nList steps to make progress on these tasks, and earmark possible dates in your calendar to work on these tasks\n\nWhat do you need and want to accomplish for each class today?\n\nGuesstimate how much time might you need to allocate for each task, and draft a daily schedule\n\nWould it be helpful to have an accountability partner to help you stay productive and on track?\n\nIf a parent, friend or sibling can play this role, think about what this might look like: texts, in-person reporting?\n\nPractice regular communication\n\nCommunication will be critical to stay connected to all parts of your academic life while you’re away from campus.\n\nAre you familiar with how each of your professors use Canvas?
