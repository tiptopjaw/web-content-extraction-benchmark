The below allowances for online students are estimates and may vary depending on students’ individual spending decisions. These allowances are “indirect,” meaning they are not charged directly to student accounts. These indirect charges are factored into your financial aid budget to ensure you can apply for enough aid for the academic year to assist with educational and living expenses. You will also see these indirect charges listed on your Financial Aid Offers. Direct charges that are applied to student accounts are listed above in the Online Program Costs sections.\n\nCosts of Attendance Allowance Definitions\n\nLiving Expenses (Food and Housing):  An allowance for food and housing costs, as determined by the institution, to be incurred by the student attending the institution on at least a half-time basis, including:\n\nA standard food allowance that provides the equivalent of three meals each day, regardless of whether a student chooses institutionally owned or operated food services (i.e., board or meal plans). Institutions must provide an allowance for purchasing food off campus for a student that does not elect institutionally owned or operated food services.\n\nHousing allowances for students residing in institutionally owned or operated housing with or without dependents must be based on the average or median amount assessed to such residents for housing charges, whichever is greater.\n\nHousing allowances for students living off-campus must include rent or other housing costs.\n\nFor dependent students living at home with parents, institutions must include a reasonable standard allowance for living expenses that are not zero.\n\nFor students living in housing on a military base or for which they receive a basic allowance under section 403(b) of title 37, United States Code, institutions must include a reasonable allowance for food on-campus or off-campus but cannot include housing costs\n\nFor all other students, institutions must include a reasonable allowance based on expenses incurred by such students.\n\nFees:  Along with tuition, an amount normally assessed to a student carrying the same academic workload, as determined by the institution.\n\nLoan Fees: An allowance for the cost of any Federal student loan fee, origination fee, or insurance premium charged to the student or the parent of the student. The allowance may be actual or average costs, as appropriate, and may not include the cost associated with non-Federal loans.\n\nBooks, Course Materials, Supplies, and Equipment:  An allowance for books, course materials, and equipment, which must include all such costs required of all students in the same course of study, including a reasonable allowance for the rental or upfront purchase of a personal computer, as determined by the institution.\n\nPersonal/Miscellaneous Expenses: An allowance, as determined by the institution, for a student attending the institution on at least a half-time basis.\n\nTransportation:  An allowance, as determined by the institution, which may include transportation between campus, residences, and the place of work.\n\nDiscounts\n\nLiberty University is committed to providing world-class education and helping students achieve their educational goals.
