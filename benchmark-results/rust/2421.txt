Picture by Vincent Court.\n\nFor Vincent Court Manager Alan Petty, the recently installed vege pods represent far more than a visual upgrade.\n\n\"It gives residents a real sense of pride and purpose,\" Mr Petty said.\n\n\"They enjoy the outdoors, and it gives them independence and true satisfaction when they can literally see the fruits of their labour.\"\n\nThe sense of fulfilment is deeply felt by resident Maureen Blair, whose passion for gardening is rooted in her childhood. The 76-year-old grew up on a vegetable farm, where hard work was part of her daily life.\n\n\"As kids, we would farm before the sun came up, then we went to school, and by 4pm we were back in the garden with mum and dad,\" Mrs Blair said.\n\nAfter losing her husband Dennis 18 years ago, Maureen said she struggled with loneliness and depression, but moving to Vincent Court two years ago marked a turning point.\n\n\"To me, it's my happy place. If something is troubling me, I head straight to the garden, and I know that will help clear my head, and it brings me great joy,\" she said.\n\n\"I think of Dennis every minute of every day, and when I'm in the garden, I hear him telling me what to do, how to do it and whether I'm doing it right.\"\n\nMrs Bair said the secret to a great garden mirrors that of a great marriage; it takes patience, tender love and care.\n\nMaureen Blair and George Holden are thrilled with the new vege pods. Picture by Vincent Court.\n\nAlongside Mrs Blair is fellow resident George Holden; the pair are regarded as the driving force behind the garden's success.\n\nMr Holden brings a lifetime of gardening knowledge and a sharp sense of humour to the veggie patch.\n\n\"I'm all about the veggies, you can't eat flowers,\" Mr Holden said.\n\n\"But Maureen has the Midas touch when it comes to the rest.\"\n\nA former train driver, Mr Holden, is now confined to a wheelchair after losing his leg to diabetes 15 years ago, making traditional gardening difficult. The 89-year-old has found renewed joy through the raised vege pods, which are much more accessible.\n\nThe raised pods allow Mr Holden, who is in a wheelchair, easy access to the gardens. Picture by Vincent Court.\n\n\"I was over the moon when they said we were getting more pods; all my Christmases had come at once,\" he said.\n\n\"Keeping your hands busy keeps you out of trouble. It's lovely showing off my work to children, grandchildren and great-grandchildren.\"\n\nHome to 84 residents, Vincent Court's gardens are enjoyed by everyone, whether they are planting seeds or simply admiring the results.\n\nThe organic produce is grown and prepared under strict safety standards, enriching both plates and spirits.\n\nFrom little things, big things certainly do grow at Vincent Court.\n\nTributes & Funerals Notices\n\nShort, Thelma May\n2025\n\nPublished 20 Dec\n\nKesby, Janet Margaret\n2025\n\nPublished 8 Nov\n\nKesby (Ophel),, Janet Margaret\n1940 - 2025\n\nPublished 8 Nov\n\nLynch, June Helen\n2025\n\nPublished 20 Sep More Tributes & Funerals Notices\n\nOur people\n\nDaily\n\nYour morning news\n\nToday's top stories curated by our news team.\n\nWeekly\n\nNote from the Editor\n\nGet the editor's insights: what's happening & why it matters.\n\nWeekly\n\nExplore Travel\n\nEvery Saturday and Tuesday, explore destinations deals, tips & travel writing to transport you around the globe.\n\nWeekdays\n\nThe Echidna\n\nSharp.
