{"title":"Best Anime of 2025","author":"Archie Fenn","date":"2025-12-14T18:00:15+00:00","main_content":"It's nearly the end of 2025, and that can only mean one thing: taking a subjective art form like anime and giving it a quantifiable ranking (yay!). It's certainly been a strange year for anime. The winter and spring seasons delivered non-stop hit after non-stop hit, including the pop culture phenomenon that was Solo Leveling 's second season. But summer and fall fell harder than Season 3 of One-Punch Man ​​​​​​. We also saw major shows return for new seasons, including SPY X FAMILY and The Apothecary Diaries. Plus, who could forget the absolute gem that is Backstabbed in a Backwater Dungeon: My Trusted Companions Tried to Kill Me, but Thanks to the Gift of an Unlimited Gacha I Got LVL 9999 Friends and Am Out for Revenge on My Former Party Members and the World (which is, unfortunately, a real show).\n\nBut among the impressive, and occasionally questionable, anime we received this year, there are some standout favorites. Hype plays a big factor in anime's popularity and ranking on sites like My Anime List. But hype is no measure of quality, so we're setting it aside ( sorry, Solo Leveling, but your plot was as bland as cardboard). So, let's dive into the best anime of 2025!\n\n11\n\n'My Dress-Up Darling' Season 2\n\nThe seinen series that made even the most casual of anime fans interested in cosplay came back for a second season this year. Fans couldn't wait to see more of Gojo and Marin's story, while their romance slowly bloomed. Season 2 of My Dress-Up Darling doubled down on the passion for cosplay, the wacky jokes, and the R-rated humor and scenarios the pair find themselves in. My Dress-Up Darling would have made it much higher up the list. However, Season 2 rapidly slowed down the overall plot, making it feel slower and somewhat weaker than the first. Either way, we're glad CloverWorks returned to this fan favorite series.\n\n10\n\n'Kowloon Generic Romance'\n\nWhile the title suggests another slow-burning, 24-episode-long romance where it takes eight hours of screen time for the main characters to hold hands, Kowloon Generic Romance was anything but. Based on the manga by Jun Mayuzuki, the show blended a \"generic romance\" with mind-bending sci-fi and an Evangelion -esque creation floating in the sky. Kowloon Generic Romance is one of the wildest genre mashups in years, and, equipped with a '90s anime aesthetic, it's one of the best shows of the year.\n\n9\n\n'Mononoke The Movie: Chapter II - The Ashes of Rage'\n\nMany anime fans may have never taken drugs, but we imagine this is what it feels like. The abstract, expressionist Mononoke franchise continued this year with Mononoke the Movie: Chapter II - The Ashes of Rage. The sequel to 2024's Phantom in the Rain was adored by anime fans and even casual movie lovers, earning a 4/5 on Letterboxd. The second film in the new Mononoke trilogy was a tremendous middle entry that will carry the series into its third and final film in Spring 2026.\n\n8\n\n'Lazarus'\n\nShinichiro Watanabe, the man behind two of the most iconic anime of all time — Cowboy Bebop and S amurai Champloo — returned with a new original sci-fi series this year. We've already seen the musically infused director incorporate jazz and hip-hop into his work. This time, Watanabe took an electronic route, with Lazarus ' fight scenes (choreographed by John Wick 's Chad Stahelski) set to pulse-pounding scores. Add to that MAPPA's always excellent animation quality, and a thought-provoking quote about criminality and humanity's obsession with dogmatic figures, Lazarus is a must-watch if you haven't seen it already.\n\n7\n\n'CITY THE ANIMATION'\n\nThe above four shows tell great plots, interspersed with moments of comedy. City the Animation is a bonkers comedy through and through. Based on the manga by Keiichi Arawi ( Nichijou ), CITY follows a bunch of oddball characters in City, who go about their daily lives, interacting with the weird people in the city, and getting into even weirder predicaments. Imagine if Monty Python and Airplane! had an animated baby... that's CITY THE ANIMATION.\n\n6\n\n'To Be Hero X'\n\nWhile it's technically not an anime and is a donghua (Chinese animation), we're going to count it because To Be Hero X took the world of anime by storm. While Lord of Mysteries was close to making it on the list, it's To Be Hero X 's consistent quality, tonal balance between humor and dark violence, and absolutely incredible action that made it one of the best anime (donghua) of 2025. If you love The Boys, you need to check out To Be Hero X.\n\n5\n\n'Chainsaw Man – The Movie: Reze Arc'\n\nAnime TV might have been in a weird place in the summer and fall seasons, but anime movies have been absolutely thriving this year. While Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba- The Movie - Infinity Castle broke anime box office records, Chainsaw Man - The Movie: Reze Arc absolutely wowed viewers. The next chapter of Tatsuki Fujimoto's anime adaptation saw major staff changes behind the scenes and a visual overhaul. While the style of Season 1 certainly had its fans (this writer included), Reze Arc was visually stunning, while expanding the \"Reze Arc\" from the manga into an impressive feature-length story.\n\n4\n\n'Dandadan' Season 2\n\nDandadan was going to be on this list somewhere. Science SARU continued proving its talents by bringing the Cursed House/Evil Eye arc of Yukinobu Tatsu's shōnen manga. Season 2 leaned even harder into the wacky comedy and emotional plot, while bringing Momo and Okarun's hunt for the latter's golden \"balls\" to the screen with stunning colors and animation. Dandadan is an anime that will be talked about for years to come, and Season 2 continued that legacy.\n\n3\n\n'Hyakuemu' ('100 Meters')\n\nArguably the sleeper hit of 2025, Hyakuemu never stood a chance at the box office against Demon Slayer and Chainsaw Man. But it rivaled (and even surpassed) the two shōnen giants in terms of quality. Based on the manga by Orb: On the Movements of the Earth 's, Uoto, Hyakuemu is a timeless sports/drama rivalry story with a healthy dose of existentialism. Studio Rock 'n' Roll Mountain (such a good name!) brought the manga to life on the big screen, with a unique use of rotoscoping to make the emotions and movements of the characters feel as realistic as possible. If you missed Hyakuemu in theaters, do yourself a favor and check it out when it comes to streaming.\n\n2\n\n'The Summer Hikaru Died'\n\nNetflix might have delivered big hits this year with the likes of Dandadan, Sakamoto Days, and the underrated Moonrise, but the streamer's best series of the year (and the second-best anime of the year) was undoubtedly The Summer Hikaru Died. Cygames Pictures delivered a hell of a debut anime series that blended LGBTQ+ themes with grotesque body horror. It's one of the rare horror anime that actually feels scary (which, as any anime fan knows, is incredibly hard to pull off)."}
