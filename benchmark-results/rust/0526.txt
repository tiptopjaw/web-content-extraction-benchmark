{"title":"Small Business Certification Programs","author":null,"date":null,"main_content":"Miami-Dade County supports the growth and success of small businesses through a range of certification programs that promote participation in County contracting opportunities\n\nCertified firms may qualify under one or more of the following categories:\n\nSmall Business Enterprise – Architectural & Engineering (SBE-A&E)\n\nSmall Business Enterprise – Construction (SBE-CON)\n\nSmall Business Enterprise – Goods and Services (SBE-GS)\n\nLocal Developing Business (LDB)\n\nDisadvantaged Business Enterprise (DBE)\n\nAirport Concessionaire Disadvantaged Business Enterprise (ACDBE)\n\nCertification for a SBE firm is valid for a three-year period. To validate continuing eligibility, SBD may conduct a random audit within the three-year certification period. Additional information may be obtained at the Small Business Enterprise Programs or call the Small Business Development office at 305-375-3111.\n\nApply for SBE Certification Programs online through the Business Management Workforce System.\n\nFor questions or assistance, contact the SPD Certification Unit at 305-375-3111 or [email protected].\n\nCertification Program Details\n\nDesigned for firms providing goods or services other than construction, architectural, or engineering services.\n\nSmall Business Enterprise – Goods\nEligibility Criteria:\n\nMust have an active Miami-Dade County Local Business Tax (LBT) receipt for at least one year\n\nMust have a physical location in Miami-Dade County (no virtual offices). Home-based offices are required to meet all zoning requirements for Miami-Dade County or the municipality where the firm's office is located\n\nPersonal net worth for each owner must not exceed $3.5 million\n\nAverage gross receipts over the past 3 years must not exceed $8 million\n\nFor manufacturers: maximum of 100 employees; for wholesalers: maximum of 50 employees\n\nLicense holder or qualifier must own at least 10% of the firm, if applicable\n\nOne active SBE certification per business owner or group\n\nSmall Business Enterprise – Services\nEligibility Criteria:\n\nMust have an active Miami-Dade County Local Business Tax (LBT) receipt for at least one year\n\nMust have a physical location in Miami-Dade County (no virtual offices). Home-based offices are required to meet all zoning requirements for Miami-Dade County or the Municipality where the firm's office is located\n\nPersonal net worth for each owner must not exceed $3.5 million\n\nAverage gross receipts over the past 3 years must not exceed $8 million\n\nLicense holder or qualifier must own at least 10% of the firm, if applicable\n\nOne active SBE certification per business owner or group.\n\nFor independent architecture, engineering, mapping, or landscape architecture firms.\n\nEligibility Criteria:\n\nMust have an active Miami-Dade County Local Business Tax (LBT) receipt for at least one year\n\nMust have a physical location in Miami-Dade County (no virtual offices).  Home-based offices are required to meet all zoning requirements for Miami-Dade County or the municipality where the firm's office is located\n\nPersonal net worth for each owner must not exceed $3.5 million\n\nAverage gross receipts over the past 3 years must not exceed $6.5 million and $8 million for engineering, surveying and mapping services, and landscape architecture services\n\nLicense holder or qualifier must own at least 25% of the firm\n\nOne active SBE certification per business owner or group\n\nFor independent construction firms performing general, heavy, or specialty trade work.\n\nEligibility Criteria:\n\nMust have an active Miami-Dade County Local Business Tax (LBT) receipt for at least one year\n\nMust have a physical location in Miami-Dade County (no virtual offices). Home-based offices are required to meet all zoning requirements for Miami-Dade County or the Municipality where the firm's office is located\n\nPersonal net worth for each owner must not exceed $3.5 million\n\nAverage gross receipts over the past 3 years must not exceed the following:\n\nGeneral contractors (NAICS 236/SIC 15): $15 million\n\nHeavy construction (NAICS 237/SIC 16): $12 million\n\nSpecialty trade (NAICS 238/SIC 17): $8 million\n\nLicense holder or qualifier must own at least 10% of the firm\n\nOne active SBE certification per business owner or group\n\nThe Local Developing Business Program is designed for firms providing aeronautical services at Miami International Airport.\n\nThis program is a gender and race-neutral program for small businesses that have non-exclusive permits to provide general aeronautical services to commercial airlines and aircraft operators at Miami International Airport.\n\nThe Disadvantaged Business Enterprise Program is a federal program that ensures equal opportunity in transportation contracting markets, addresses the effects of discrimination in transportation contracting, and promotes increased participation in federally funded contracts by small, socially and economically disadvantaged businesses, including enterprises owned by minorities and women.\n\nThe purpose of the ACDBE program is to increase opportunities for minority and women-owned small businesses to operate as concessionaires in airports around the country. The ACDBE program for airport concessions is implemented under Title 49 Code of Federal Regulations Part 23 (49 CFR 23). Florida's certified ACDBE firms are also listed in FDOT's UCP DBE Directory.\n\nApplying, Renewing and Requesting\n\nTo apply for or renew certificate(s):\n\nLog into the Business Management Workforce System\n\nUnder Certified Firms, select \"Apply for/Renew Certification\"\n\nSelect the options that are appropriate for your company\n\nAnswer the eligibility questions\n\nPreview the document list and select \"Continue\" to the application\n\nSelect \"Process\" to complete each section\n\nA green arrow will indicate that the section is complete.
