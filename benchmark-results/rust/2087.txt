In this section, we'll explore the 5 types of restaurant services and their characteristics.\n\nTable Service: \n\nTable service is a common type of restaurant service all over the world. In this type of service, customers are seated at a table and served by a waiter or waitress. Servers take customer orders, proceed with the food service, and provide assistance during meals. This type of service is often used in a formal or upscale restaurant, which focuses on providing an enjoyable dining experience.\n\nCounter Service: \n\nCounter-service restaurants usually have a small menu of prepared foods, such as sandwiches, salads, and coffee which are ordered and paid for at the counter. Customers can then bring their food to the table or eat on the go. Counter-service restaurants are often fast and convenient, with a focus on efficiency.\n\nSelf-service: \n\nIn self-service restaurants, customers order and pay for their food at a counter or kiosk and serve themselves. This type of service is common in fast-food restaurants, cafeterias, and some coffee shops. \n\nSelf-service allows customers to have more control over their dining experience. In Indonesia, several restaurant chains use this service concept, one of which is Marugame Udon.\n\nBuffet Service:\n\nBuffet restaurants offer a self-service dining experience, where customers serve themselves from a variety of dishes displayed on a table or counter. Prices are often based on a flat fee, not per dish, and customers can usually return for additional servings. A lot of businesses in the hospitality industry use this service, including hotels and restaurants. \n\nBut, not every restaurant uses this concept in full.
