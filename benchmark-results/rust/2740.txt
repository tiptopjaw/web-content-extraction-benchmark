{"title":"Digital Photo Frame App Makes Old Hardware Useful Again","author":"AppAddict","date":"2025-12-19T20:20:42+00:00","main_content":"December 19th, 2025\n\nI love it when I find an app that solves a problem I didn't know I\nhad. I am always reluctant to get rid of any Apple hardware that still\nhas any life left in it. Sometimes finding a practical use for an aging\niPad or Intel Mac can be challenging. But now that I've discovered what\nI think is the best in its class app for displaying digital photography,\nI think Aura Photo Frames are great gadgets, but they are expensive and\nhave limitations. Macs and iPads generally have better displays. They\nhave network connectivity built in, and using one of them with the right\nsoftware gives you a huge variety of photo sources. You can even use the\napp on an Apple TV to do things you can't do natively with the built-in\napp.\n\nWhen I have company coming over, I make slide shows featuring my guests\nand set them to play. Anyone who needs a display for a trade show or\npresentation can use this app a lot more easily than setting up a\nrotating PowerPoint or Keynote presentation. I also like to make\nslideshows of trips I go on to keep the memories alive after I return\nhome.\n\nI use Digital\nPhoto Frame and Slideshow, a universal app that works on the last\nsix versions of macOS (Catalina-Sequoia) and iOS/iPadOS (iOS12-iOS26).\nThe app has over 1K App Store reviews and a 4.8 rating. It's been\nfeatured by Apple in \"Apps We Love.\" You can read plenty of testimonials\nand get support at the\ndeveloper's website.. I contacted him while working on this review.\nBased on my interaction and his reputation among users, you should have\nno problem getting help should you need it or getting a response if you\nhave a feature request.  I asked about adding support for WebDAV  cloud\nservices and he let me know he's working on adding that support (and for\nother services available through the Files app). Digital Photo Frame is\nupdated frequently.\n\nPhoto Sources and Display Controls\n\nInclude: local photo roll, albums, and smart albums\n\nCan also use iCloud photos and shared albums\n\nSyncing services like Google Photos & Flickr\n\nSupports Unsplash\n\nDisplays live photos and videos\n\nInterval times range from three seconds to 24 hours\n\nPhotos can be shuffled or displayed in a fixed order\n\nMultiple transition styles, including the Ken Burns effect\n\nPhotos can be in portrait or landscape mode\n\nInformation Overlays\n\nCurrent date and time\n\nCurrent weather\n\nUV index and air quality reports\n\nDigital Photo Frame and Slideshow includes timers to start and stop\nslideshows automatically. The Apple Photos slideshow feature lacks audio\ncontinuity and detailed timing controls.\n\nRecent Updates\n\nIf you've used the app in the past and moved on to other options, you\nshould take a look at some of the recent developments. Version 6\nintroduced:\n\nOffline slideshow support that allows you to cache photos so you can run without network connectivity\n\nThe Apple TV app\n\nImproved shuffle behavior and album sorting\n\nMore localized language support\n\nMy Favorite Features\n\nSo easy to use that my youngest grandchildren can design their own slideshows\n\nI can keep music playing in the background (unlike with the native Apple app)\n\nThe whole family can add photos to slideshows by contributing to shared albums\n\nAffordable, But Not Cheap\n\nThere's a lifetime purchase option and a subscription option. You can\ntry out the app on all your compatible devices for as little as $2.99\nfor a month. A yearly subscription is currently $29.99, although,\naccording to AppRaven, there have been sales for as low as $12.99 a\nyear. If you just want to buy the app outright, knowing that you should\nbe able to use it for years to come on multiple devices, it's $69.99.\n\nIf you have hardware that's so old you can't run a compatible version of\na Mac operating system on it, try Digikam,\na FOSS photo management app. I use it on two 2009 iMacs. One runs Debian\nand the other Xubuntu, but you can run older versions of Digikam on\nvintage Mac operating systems."}
