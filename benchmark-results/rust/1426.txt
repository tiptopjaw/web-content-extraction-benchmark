Your cyber security hygiene helps you better defend your networks, systems and data from threat actors.\n\nThreat actors, even in more sophisticated attacks, leverage common vulnerabilities and weaknesses to attack systems and gain initial access. By building a solid cyber security foundation, your organization is better positioned to protect, defend and recover from cyber incidents.\n\nOn this page\n\nCyber security hygiene checklist\n\nCyber security hygiene checklist\n\nThe following checklist provides actions your organization can take to strengthen your cyber security.\n\nWhile not all actions may be feasible, you should prioritize implementing those that are most impactful and sustainable for your organization. Doing so will enhance your cyber security posture.\n\nNetwork and endpoint protection\n\nProtect your network and endpoints with the following tools\n\nanti-virus and anti-malware software\n\nnetwork protocol inspection tools\n\nendpoint detection and response\n\nfirewalls\n\nwireless intrusion detection and prevention systems\n\nmobile endpoint threat management solutions and mobile threat defence products\n\nSegment your networks to stop traffic from flowing to sensitive or restricted zones\n\nImplement a security information and event management system to enable real-time, continuous monitoring to identify anomalies in your\n\nnetwork traffic\n\nwireless access points\n\nmobile device gateways\n\nMonitor your security critical components, including the\n\nDomain Name System (DNS) server\n\nauthentication server\n\npublic key infrastructure\n\nImplement protective DNS to prevent users from inadvertently visiting potentially malicious domains on the Internet\n\nRegularly renew cryptographic keys to maintain secure communications\n\nDocument secure baseline configurations for all your IT, operational technology components and cloud infrastructure\n\nEstablish and maintain a configuration management database\n\nConduct and maintain an inventory of your IT assets\n\nManage and detect unauthorized assets by developing and maintaining IT asset management procedures that ensure proper tagging and labelling of hardware and software assets\n\nRead more\n\nPreventative security tools (ITSAP.00.058)\n\nUsing security information and event management tools to manage cyber protection\n\nEnable automatic updates and patches for your firmware, hardware, software and operating systems, especially for Internet-exposed services and systems\n\nPatch operating systems and applications promptly after assessing organizational risk and confirming compatibility with your environment\n\nEnforce phishing-resistant multi-factor authentication (MFA) for all accounts and systems, especially those with administrative privileges\n\nEncourage the use of strong, unique, and confidential passphrases or passwords where MFA is not technically feasible\n\nEnsure administrators use dedicated workstations that do not allow web browsing or email access\n\nRegularly review and update user privileges, such as\n\nremove users no longer in your organization\n\nedit user privileges if users no longer require access to certain data or systems\n\nlimit administrative privileges to a small number of users\n\nrequire two-person integrity for administrative privileges\n\nconduct administrative functions from a dedicated administrative workstation\n\nApply the principle of least privilege, ensuring users only have the set of privileges that are essential to performing authorized tasks\n\nConsider role-based access control\n\nManage mobile devices with unified endpoint management software\n\nImplement application allow lists to control what applications and components are allowed on your networks and systems\n\nAssess third-party applications to identify and disable unnecessary components or functions or require human intervention before activation (for example, macros)\n\nDisable autorun or autoplay on all your operating systems and web browsers to avoid automatic installations of unauthorized software\n\nEstablish an incident response plan and conduct annual tests to ensure timely restoration of critical functions and effective recovery\n\nCategorize your assets to identify those that are most critical to your organization's operations\n\nRegularly backup critical data and systems to offline storage, ensuring backups are isolated from network connections\n\nTest your backups periodically to ensure data and systems can be recovered quickly and successfully\n\nProactively manage device lifecycles to address vulnerabilities in end-of-life or end-of-service-life devices, which often remain unpatched and
