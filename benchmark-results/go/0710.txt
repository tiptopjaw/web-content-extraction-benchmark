Let’s dive into how this works and what might be hiding in your pipes. Table of Contents The Basics: What Is a Plumbing Camera Inspection? Plumbing camera inspections utilize a small waterproof camera attached to a flexible rod. This device is carefully inserted into your plumbing system, sending live video footage to a monitor. Plumbers use this technology to inspect pipes, identify blockages, and detect damage with pinpoint accuracy. Unlike traditional methods that rely on educated guesses, plumbing camera inspections provide a clear, real-time view of hidden plumbing issues. Whether it’s a tree root invasion or a cracked pipe, this non-invasive approach allows for efficient diagnosis and targeted solutions—all without unnecessary digging or dismantling. Common Hidden Issues Found in Pipes Your pipes might seem fine from the outside, but inside, all kinds of problems can develop. Here are some of the most common hidden plumbing issues uncovered during plumbing camera inspections: Tree Root Infiltration Tree roots are naturally drawn to moisture, making underground pipes an easy target. Over time, roots can penetrate small cracks in pipes, leading to blockages, slow drainage, and even pipe breakage. Pipe Corrosion & Deterioration Older pipes, especially those made of metal, can corrode and weaken. This leads to leaks, reduced water flow, and, eventually, pipe failure. Without an inspection, these issues often remain unnoticed until serious damage occurs. Buildup of Grease, Hair, and Debris Everyday waste like grease , soap scum, and hair can accumulate inside pipes, restricting water flow and causing persistent clogs. A plumbing camera inspection reveals the extent of buildup so it can be properly cleared before it leads to major backups. Foreign Objects & Lost Items Believe it or not, plumbers frequently discover unexpected items inside pipes. Whether it’s a child’s toy, a piece of jewelry, or something accidentally flushed down the toilet, these obstructions can cause stubborn clogs and damage over time. Undetected Leaks & Structural Damage Small leaks might not seem like a big deal, but they can escalate into major problems. Hidden leaks weaken pipes, promote mold growth, and increase water bills. Plumbing camera inspections pinpoint these leaks early, preventing costly repairs down the line.
