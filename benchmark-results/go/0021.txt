It was mostly following the link in my original post. However, there were a couple minor differences.</p> <p>To extract the .cap file, the article mentioned using a <code>innoextract</code>. However, that tool didn’t work as it wasn’t a supported file type. What I did find, though, was that the .exe was just a self-extracting zip file. So, I was able to <code>unzip</code> to get the files out.</p> <p>I then followed the instructions from the article by:<br> Running <code>fwupdmgr get-devices</code> to get a list of devices and, specifically, get the GUID for the system.<br> Running <code>sudo fwupdmgr install-blob {path to .cap} {GUID}</code>.<br> Then reboot, chew fingernails, and hope. It rebooted into the BIOS Flash and did its thing. Confirmed running the new version.</p> <p>Thank you all for trying to help.
