Understanding Furniture Quality Before you discover the sea of designs and styles, you need to understand what quality is for a piece of furniture. The materials used to produce a piece of furniture show its quality. Hardwoods such as maple, teak, and oak are well-known for their endurance and are used to make such items (Homes Direct 365, 2020). Note the signs of solid wood construction on wooden furniture: natural grain patterns and weight. Craftsmanship should be flawless, with tight, seamless joints and drawers that easily slide in and out. The frame must be solid, and cushions should have a high foam density to retain their shape over time. Finishes and Functionality A quality finish enhances the piece’s longevity and improves its appearance. The finish should be even and smoothed, with no rough patches or spots. Functional components like adjustable shelves or reclining mechanisms should operate correctly, adding to the furniture’s ease and comfort.
