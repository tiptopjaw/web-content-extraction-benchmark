What are the most important oral hygiene products I need? The cornerstones of any good oral hygiene routine are a soft-bristled toothbrush (manual or electric), fluoride toothpaste, and dental floss. For many of our patients in Surprise, an antiseptic or fluoride mouthwash can also provide extra protection against plaque and cavities. 2. How often should I replace my toothbrush? The American Dental Association (ADA) recommends replacing your toothbrush every three to four months, or sooner if the bristles become frayed or matted. Worn-out bristles are less effective at cleaning your teeth and can even damage your gums. 3. Is an electric toothbrush really better than a manual one? Both types of toothbrushes can be effective if used correctly. However, many people find that electric toothbrushes make it easier to brush for the recommended two minutes and clean more thoroughly. At West Bell Dental Care, we often recommend them for patients who struggle with technique or have limited dexterity. 4. I have sensitive teeth. What kind of products should I use? If you have sensitive teeth, look for a toothpaste specifically formulated with ingredients like potassium nitrate or stannous fluoride. You should also use an ultra-soft toothbrush and a gentle, alcohol-free mouthwash to avoid irritation. We can provide personalized recommendations during your check-up. 5.
