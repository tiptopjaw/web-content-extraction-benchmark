Tips for budget savvy travelers on cheap tours Book flights in advance Book as far in advance as possible (airlines release their seats up to one year in advance ) Book your return ticket together with the outbound flight If you don’t know your return date, book a flexible return ticket . Rescheduling is usually cheaper Flights during the week are often cheaper than over the weekend Travel during off-season Tour operators offer organized adventures during the off-season at a reduced rate Especially in Europe and Asia, destinations are less crowded On-site attractions lure visitors with discounts Bonus: It's always off-season somewhere on our beautiful planet Save before you start New York Times best-selling author and financial expert David Bach says the best strategy is to make saving “automatic.” Open an account, sub-account, or wallet specifically for your trip Deposit a fixed amount each month or week—the easiest way is to let it debit automatically Of course, the good old piggy bank works, too Additional tips Travel outside of school vacation periods Use local public transport instead of a private driver or taxi Use apps to compare prices , such as Skyscanner, Hopper, GasBuddy, and more Cook for yourself instead of always eating out When you eat out, dine where the locals eat .
