View Academic Catalog What is the Software Development track? This specialization trains students to build robust software solutions across platforms—covering database design, multimedia/web, mobile apps, software engineering, and large-scale project development. How many credits are required to graduate? Students must complete 120 total semester credit hours, including 25 credits of core CS, 19 in the Software Development track, 17 in mathematics, and general education/electives. At least 48 upper-level credits and 30 final credits must be earned at UTPB. Are there specific grade requirements? Yes—students must earn a minimum grade of C in all major courses and maintain an overall GPA of at least 2.0 to meet graduation requirements. What are the key courses in this track? Major upper-level track courses include: COSC 4415: Database Systems COSC 4455: Multimedia & Web Development COSC 4460: Software Engineering COSC 4485: Mobile Application Development COSC 4395: Research/Capstone project How much math is required? Students complete 17 credit hours in math, including Calculus I & II, Intro to Probability, and Mathematical Reasoning—or substitute equivalent courses. Is hands-on experience included in the curriculum? Absolutely. Students engage in practical, lab-based projects and complete the COSC 4395 capstone, emphasizing real-world software development and portfolio-building. What career opportunities does this track support? Graduates are prepared for careers as Software Developers, Database Administrators, Systems Analysts, Front‑End/Web Developers, Mobile App Engineers, and more.
