Bathroom Remodel: Where Function Meets Spa Vibes This tiny room packs a major punch in terms of ROI and impact. Living Room Remodel: Style Meets Comfort This is your everyday space—the Netflix zone, the after-dinner chill zone, the “where everyone ends up anyway” zone. A great living room remodel can dramatically improve both your home’s vibe and functionality. Popular Living Room Remodel Ideas Create an open-concept space by removing non-structural walls Add a feature wall (wood paneling, wallpaper, or a bold paint color) Install recessed lighting or a statement chandelier Upgrade flooring to hardwood, engineered wood, or LVP Add built-in shelving around a fireplace or media unit Cost Breakdown Type of Remodel Average Cost Timeline Cosmetic (paint, flooring, lights) $7,000 2–3 weeks Mid-Range (built-ins, fireplace) $15,000 4–5 weeks High-End (structural changes, layout rework) $30,000+ 6+ weeks Pros Instant visual upgrade to your most-used room Creates a cozy, inviting space for family and guests Great for entertaining and resale Cons May need permits if you’re modifying layout Structural changes = longer timeline and more $$ 4. Bedroom Remodel: Your Recharge Zone Your bedroom should feel like a sanctuary, not a leftover space. A well-designed bedroom remodel can dramatically improve sleep quality and overall peace of mind. Bedroom Remodel Ideas Upgrade lighting to include dimmable fixtures or sconces Add crown molding or ceiling detail for visual interest Walk-in closet conversions or expansions Custom built-ins or under-bed storage Soundproofing for better sleep Cost Breakdown Type of Remodel Average Cost Timeline Cosmetic (paint, carpet, decor) $5,000 1–2 weeks Mid-Range (flooring, closet rework) $10,000 2–4 weeks High-End (layout changes, custom millwork) $20,000+ 4+ weeks Pros Improves comfort and sleep environment Can increase home value with better closet space Low-disruption if done in phases Cons High-end finishes add up quickly Usually the least urgent remodel, so it’s easy to deprioritize 5. Must-Have Mud Room Features Built-in cubbies and bench seating Hooks for coats, backpacks, and wet gear Tile or waterproof flooring Overhead cabinetry or lockers Charging station for phones/tablets Cost Breakdown Type of Remodel Average Cost Timeline Basic Add-On (bench + hooks) $3,000 1 week Mid-Range (cubbies, tile, lighting) $7,000 2–3 weeks Custom Mud Room Build $15,000+ 3–4 weeks Pros Keeps daily clutter out of your living spaces Adds resale value, especially in family homes Cons Needs strategic placement—ideally near garage or backdoor May require space you don’t currently have 8. Outdoor Space Remodel: Bring the Indoors Out Post-pandemic, outdoor spaces became essential. It’s your extra living room—with fresh air and fewer crumbs. If your whole house needs love, don’t just grab a sledgehammer and start swinging. Here’s the smartest order to tackle renovations — based on impact, efficiency, and staying sane: Suggested Remodeling Order Structural + Safety Fixes Foundation, roofing, plumbing, electrical No point having a sexy kitchen if your roof leaks on it Layout Changes + Major Construction Removing walls, adding rooms, expanding square footage Messy, noisy, dusty — do it before you paint or refloor Kitchen and Bathrooms High ROI, daily use, major impact Usually need plumbing/electrical work, so do these early Living Room, Bedrooms, Mudrooms Less disruptive, easier to phase in Cosmetic upgrades, built-ins, storage, flooring Finishes + Paint Always do paint and trim last (to avoid redoing it after other trades mess it up) Outdoor Upgrades Landscaping, patios, lighting Do it after all that heavy interior construction is over Pro tip: If you’re living at home during renovations, do one floor or section at a time so you don’t lose your mind (or your socks). 7 Signs It’s Time to Remodel Still on the fence about remodeling?
