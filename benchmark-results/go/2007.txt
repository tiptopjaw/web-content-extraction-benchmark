Let’s delve into the top interior design trends for 2025, what’s fading out, and how to incorporate these elements into your designs. In with the New: The Defining Interior Design Trends of 2025 1. Warm and Earthy Colors: Comfort in Every Corner Leading the charge in interior design trends for 2025, earthy colors are transforming homes into sanctuaries of warmth and serenity. Chocolate brown takes center stage, accompanied by deep reds, burgundies, and natural tones like sage green and soft blues. These hues evoke calmness while keeping spaces grounded and inviting, offering a more intimate and organic atmosphere . Expanded Impact: Earthy colors are more than just an aesthetic choice: they reflect a growing desire for spaces that connect to the natural world. By incorporating natural materials like wood, stone, and linen alongside these tones, the interior design trends become a reflection of a more sustainable and mindful approach to living. Incorporation Tip: Think about a chocolate-brown accent wall paired with emerald green furniture. Alternatively, use burgundy throw pillows or a sage-toned rug to subtly infuse these colors. Earthy-colored wallpapers and textured fabrics are also perfect for creating depth and richness in your designs. 2. Nostalgic Revival: The ’20s and ’30s Return Vintage styles from the 1920s and 1930s are making a bold comeback in 2025. Art Deco ’s geometric elegance and Bauhaus minimalism are particularly popular, as they bring a sense of craftsmanship and character to modern interiors. These interior design trends emphasize a perfect blend of form and function , combining intricate detailing with modern practicality. Expanded Impact: The return to vintage styles taps into a collective desire for something timeless and luxurious. Art Deco lighting , sculptural furniture, and sleek materials like lacquered wood and polished metal can transport spaces to an era that values both craftsmanship and opulence. Incorporation Tip: Combine vintage-inspired lighting with sleek, contemporary furniture. A Bauhaus armchair or an Art Deco chandelier can serve as a conversation-starting centerpiece in your living room or dining area. 3. Maximalism and Bold Patterns Take Center Stage The era of minimalism is fading, and maximalism is taking over. Bold prints, vibrant colors, and eclectic furniture combinations define this year’s interior design trends. This trend celebrates personal expression through bold patterns, rich textures , and layers of eclecticism, bringing joy and energy to every corner of your home. Expanded Impact: Maximalism encourages you to tell your story through design — each piece, from animal prints to geometric patterns , has meaning and significance. The boldness of maximalism creates spaces that feel alive, reflecting the personality and tastes of those who inhabit them. Incorporation Tip: Layer patterns through textiles and wall treatments. A leopard-print ottoman or a floral wallpapered accent wall adds life and personality to any room. Mix bold and subtle pieces to prevent overwhelming the space while embracing the joyful spirit of maximalism . 4. Curved and Organic Shapes: Soft Elegance Curves are dominating furniture and architectural design in 2025. From rounded sofas to circular coffee tables, these organic forms introduce softness and elegance , enhancing both comfort and style. These interior design trends create a fluid, harmonious atmosphere that invites relaxation and balance. Expanded Impact: Curved furniture can soften harsh angles and create a sense of calm . The popularity of organic shapes in interior design trends suggests a return to spaces that are more inviting and less rigid. Curves also help with space planning, making areas feel more expansive and less cluttered. Incorporation Tip: Opt for a curved sofa to anchor your living room or use round mirrors to soften the sharp lines of other furniture. Circular rugs or arched doorways are subtle yet effective ways to incorporate these organic shapes into your designs. 5. Classic Glass Lighting: Timeless Elegance Lighting continues to be a transformative element in interior design. Murano glass pendants and chandeliers are among the most sought-after fixtures for 2025. These interior design trends combine artistry with functionality, offering an elegant, vintage-inspired lighting option that adds an element of timeless sophistication to any space. Expanded Impact: Glass lighting brings both form and function, offering light that is soft, diffused, and ethereal. As we move further into 2025, incorporating glass lighting is a way to infuse luxury and a touch of the past into contemporary interiors. Murano glass and similar artisan-crafted pieces are an investment in quality and beauty. Incorporation Tip: Install Murano glass pendants above a kitchen island or use a statement chandelier in the dining room for a touch of vintage-inspired sophistication. Glass lighting can also serve as a focal point, drawing the eye and transforming the ambiance of a room. 6. Functional Elegance in Kitchens and Beyond Kitchens are more than just places to cook — they are multifunctional hubs for gathering, socializing, and even working. Sculleries, outdoor kitchens, and unique additions like home pizza ovens are becoming key features in 2025’s interior design trends. The focus is on creating spaces that are as functional as they are stylish. Expanded Impact: The evolution of the kitchen reflects the shift in how we use our homes. Kitchens are now spaces where we not only prepare meals but also entertain , relax, and work. These multifunctional spaces demand innovative design solutions, from smart appliances to custom storage. Incorporation Tip: Create an outdoor prep station for entertaining or add bespoke shelving in the kitchen for both style and functionality. Consider incorporating sustainable materials for countertops and cabinetry, aligning with the overall eco-conscious direction of 2025 interior design trends . 7. Sustainability and Personalization Lead the Way Sustainability is no longer a passing trend but a fundamental aspect of interior design in 2025. Eco-friendly materials, handcrafted furniture , and repurposed items are defining the interior design trends of the year. The emphasis is on creating spaces that not only look good but also contribute to a more sustainable, ethical lifestyle. Expanded Impact: Sustainability in interior design reflects a global shift towards environmental consciousness. As more designers and homeowners seek to reduce their carbon footprint, incorporating recycled materials, locally sourced items, and energy-efficient solutions is more important than ever. These practices are becoming integral to creating thoughtful , responsible designs. Incorporation Tip: Choose recycled woods for furniture or incorporate handcrafted textiles to add character and reduce your carbon footprint. Use sustainable materials such as bamboo, cork, and stone, and invest in long-lasting, high-quality pieces that will stand the test of time. Out with the Old: Trends Falling Out of Favor As we embrace new interior design trends, certain styles are taking a backseat: Home Offices: Dedicated home office renovations are decreasing as hybrid workspaces shift toward multifunctional areas. Midcentury Modernism: Once a staple, midcentury designs are losing their appeal. Iconic pieces like the Hans Wegner Wishbone chair have seen their popularity decline. Barbie Pink: Bright pink tones have dropped dramatically in favor, replaced by earthy and natural palettes. Dark Grays and Slates: These muted tones are losing out to the warmth of browns and greens. Antiques Before 1920: While vintage is in, antiques from before 1920 remain the least favored design element. Nostalgia and innovation, sustainability and self-expression — all these elements converge to create spaces that are both meaningful and stylish.
