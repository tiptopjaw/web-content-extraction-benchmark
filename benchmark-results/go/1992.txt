Here are some common red flags to watch out for when you’re choosing a web design partner: 🚩 Beautiful visuals, poor performance Some agencies know how to impress in a pitch deck, glossy mockups, slick animations, the works. Some teams overdesign pages with effects that look good on Dribbble but hurt performance, search engine optimization, or accessibility. Don't get distracted by shiny trophies; ask how their work actually performs. 🚩 Dev handoffs that break everything Design-to-development handoff is where many projects fall apart. Some agencies hand over Figma files with no context, no dev specifications, and no thought about how real developers will build it. That leads to endless fixes, rising costs, and frustrated engineering teams — a recurring complaint from teams that worked with a mobile app design agency unfamiliar with web development handoff processes. Look for an agency that collaborates with developers early, not just at the finish line. 🚩 “Yes” teams that don’t push back It feels good when an agency agrees with everything you say.. until you realize you’ve launched a generic site with no clear message.
