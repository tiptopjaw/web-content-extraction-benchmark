Order on Amazon Project management Certified Associate in Project Management (CAPM) The Project Management Institute (PMI) has been around since 1969, helping business workers develop careers in project management, mainly through professional development and certifications. Why it matters: The CAPM is ideal for up-and-coming project managers looking to establish credibility and advance their earning potential by up to 25 percent more than non-certified professionals. Requirements and format: You need to show proof of a high school diploma, GED or global equivalent. By the time you take the exam, you need to have attained 23 hours of project management education. The single exam has 150 questions and is available through Pearson VUE. Target audience: Entry-level to mid-level professionals seeking their first project management credential. Cost: $225 for PMI members or $300 for nonmembers. The certification is valid for five years and you must retake the exam to maintain the credential. Business analysis Certified Business Analysis Professional (CBAP) If you’re interested in analyzing business needs and coming up with solutions, take a look at the CBAP by the International Institute of Business Analysis (IIBA). Why it matters: The CBAP is an advanced certification aimed at managers and leaders, with strong market demand reflected in over 3,200 job postings as of Q1 2025. Requirements and format: You need at least 7,500 hours of business analysis experience over the last decade and 900 of those hours must directly relate to four of the six BABOK Guide knowledge areas. On top of that, you need at least 35 hours of professional development credits (earned over the last four years), plus two references. The CBAP exam has 120 multiple-choice questions, based on scenarios and case studies and is available through PSI. Target audience: Senior business analysts and managers with more than five years of business analysis experience. Cost: The exam costs $350 for IIBA members or $505 for nonmembers and a $145 application fee is required. To maintain the CBAP, credential holders must earn at least 60 continuing development units within three years of achieving certification. How to choose the right certification Selecting the right business certification requires careful evaluation of your career stage, industry demands and return on investment to ensure the credential aligns with your professional goals. 1. Assess your career goals. Determine whether you’re seeking entry-level credibility, mid-career advancement or executive-level recognition. Entry-level professionals should consider CAPM or free certifications, while experienced professionals benefit from PMP, CBAP or specialized vendor certifications. 2. Evaluate industry demand. Research job postings in your target market. Our analysis shows PMP leads with 24,780 postings, followed by CAPM (3,923), HubSpot (3,371), and CSCP (3,306) as of Q1 2025. 3. Consider the return on investment. Factor in both cost and potential salary impact. While CSCP requires significant investment ($1,420 to $1,975), it delivers 36 percent salary premiums. Free certifications like HubSpot provide immediate value with no financial risk. 4. Match prerequisites to your background. Ensure you meet experience requirements. CBAP requires 7,500 hours of business analysis experience, while CAPM only needs a high school diploma and 23 hours of education. 5. Plan for maintenance requirements. Consider ongoing certification requirements. PMP requires 60 PDUs every three years, while CAPM requires complete re-examination every five years.
