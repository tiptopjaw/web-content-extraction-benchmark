How Often Should Electrical Maintenance Be Done? The frequency of electrical maintenance depends on several factors including the type of electrical equipment, its usage, environmental conditions, and applicable regulations or guidelines. Generally, electrical maintenance should be performed regularly to ensure safety, reliability, and efficiency. Here are some general guidelines: Routine Inspections : Conduct regular visual inspections of electrical equipment and systems by qualified personnel to check for signs of wear, damage, or overheating. Scheduled Maintenance : Follow manufacturer-recommended maintenance schedules, adjusting for environmental conditions or heavy usage, to ensure optimal performance and safety. Regulatory Compliance : Adhere to industry regulations and standards regarding electrical maintenance to ensure legal compliance and safety. Emergency Maintenance : Address malfunctions or safety hazards promptly through immediate maintenance or repairs to prevent further damage or risk. Equipment Age : Consider the age of equipment, as older systems may require more frequent maintenance to address wear and aging components. Expert Electrical Advice for San Jose Residents If your house has a breaker box instead of a fuse box, when something goes off, it is likely due to a thrown breaker. If this is the case, you simply need to turn the breaker completely off and then turn it back on again in order to restore power. If this problem persists, you may have a bad breaker or there may be too much power being drawn from the area that it controls. Periodically, you will encounter a blown fuse. This occurs when the thin metal strip that runs through the middle of a fuse has melted. When this occurs, the fuse needs to be replaced, but make sure that your replacement is rated the same as the existing one. It is also good to perform a fuse replacement while on dry ground. When using your power tools with an extension cord, check the power rating of the item as well as the gauge of the extension cord. You could be losing precious power if the cord cannot carry enough to the device.
